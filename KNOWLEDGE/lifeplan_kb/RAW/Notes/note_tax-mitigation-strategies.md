> Beginning: Make sure Sole Proprietor LLC has filed a Form 2553 to establish tax treatment as an S-Corp. Then, the following items can help reduce your Adjusted Gross Income, or the amount of income you pay income tax on per year.

1. **Direct Business Income**
   a. Set basic CEO salary
   b. Rest is paid in distributions

2. **Home Business Office**
   a. "The Augusta Rule"
   b. IRS as Section 280A, allows homeowners to rent out their home for up to 14 days per year without needing to report the rental income on their individual tax return.
   c. Find comp's for "Office Retreat" or "Professional Office Day Rental" in your area to establish day rate.
   d. Create and illustrate invoice showing days rented by your S-Corp and cut a reimbursement check back to you individually as the homeowner for the days.

3. **Business Vehicle — Section 179 Deduction**
   a. This is accelerated depreciation and brings the value of the car's depreciation all into current year.
   b. Best Practice — have the vehicle listed in your books as a business vehicle and determine the percentage of time it is used for business purposes. (Mine for PHE is 100% because for personal use I can use my truck)
   c. The vehicle must have greater than 6,000 lb. gross weight.
   d. The calculation for expense right off is Purchase Price × % Business Use × 80% for 2023.

4. **Business Vehicle — Ongoing expenses**
   a. Because the vehicle is owned and paid for by the business — all payments, gas, tires, maintenance, insurance, etc, is ALSO a business expense (deduction against income)

5. **Business Vehicle — Clean Vehicle Credit (AGI must < $300k as family)**

6. **Hire Your Kid**
   a. In future — for now — can maximize giving — you can give up to $17,000 to as many people as you want in 2023 without having to worry about paying the federal gift tax. And, again, if you're married, your spouse can also give $17,000 to the same people.
   b. 529 Savings Plans vs. Custodial Child IRA — the contribution limit for a Roth IRA for kids in 2023 is $6,500

7. **Travel Expenses**
   a. As a consultant, anytime you travel you should list hotel, airfare, valet, business meals, rental cars and pay with company credit card, or pay personal and cut a reimbursement check to yourself for your business expenses.

8. **Cell Phone**
   a. All 12 months should be paid and in the name of S-Corp or reimburse yourself for the expenses.

9. **Computer / Internet Expenses / Educational Expenses**
   a. Pay from S-Corp card or reimburse yourself personally from S-Corp
   b. All books — seminars — lectures etc.

10. **Direct Business Expenses / Internet Expenses / Educational Expenses**
    a. Legal / Accounting bills, Contract Labor, Advertising, SM promotes, cameras, gear, home improvement project you do for making of content, office rental downtown, etc. etc.

11. **Fringe Benefits**
    a. Pay your retirement plans funding & health insurance (premiums/HAS contributions etc. from your S-Corp)
    b. R&D Tax Credits expenses related to the development of future business needs/consulting tools, and materials can be listed as a business expense.
