> **Purpose:** Reduce financial stress by separating *what you can control now* from *contingency levers* and *trigger-based plans*.

### 1) What you can control right now
- **Review cadence:** Run frequent financial reviews (cash, runway, receivables).
- **Team accountability:** Make owners and next steps explicit.
- **Offer clarity:** Refine your core product or service offering.
- **Client retention:** Improve communication, outcomes, and renewal strategy.
- **Pricing:** Audit pricing strategy and packaging.
- **Sales pipeline:** Tighten the pipeline stages and weekly activity targets.
- **Expenses:** Manage expenses proactively (cut waste early, not late).
- **Legal/compliance:** Confirm legal and compliance readiness.
- **Morale:** Protect team morale with clarity and steady leadership.
- **Personal rhythms:** Build personal stress management routines (sleep, movement, prayer, margin).

---
### 2) Contingency levers (if you need to pull them)
- Cut non-essential costs.
- Delay major expenditures.
- Access credit lines.
- Renegotiate payment terms.
- Scale down operations temporarily.
- Increase marketing effort if momentum is positive.
- Outsource or reduce staff.
- Pivot product offerings quickly.
- Seek investor support.
- Sell assets if needed.

---
### 3) "If this, then that" trigger paths (pre-decide, don't panic)
> The goal is to **decide thresholds now**, so you can execute calmly later.

| Trigger | Then do |
|---------|---------|
| Revenue drops 10% | Cut non-essential spending by **X%** and pause discretionary tools/subscriptions. |
| Revenue exceeds target threshold | Decide ahead of time what you will fund (hiring, marketing, expansion) and the cap. |
| Key client backs out | Launch a backup sales initiative immediately (outreach sprint, referrals, lead gen offer). |
| Cash reserves hit minimum threshold | Activate credit line and renegotiate payment terms to protect runway. |

---
### 4) Next pass (5 minutes)
- Define **X%** for the 10% drop scenario.
- Write the **exact threshold numbers** (weekly revenue, cash minimum, receivables limit).
- Assign 1 owner for the weekly review and 1 owner for the contingency plan.

---
If you don't set a time to think about this, you'll always think about it.

| When to Think About This | When Not to Think About This |
|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| During scheduled business reviews (e.g., weekly or monthly) | During personal/family time |
| When planning budgets or forecasts | Before bedtime |
| In strategy or leadership meetings | During meals and breaks |
| While reviewing key metrics and milestones | When engaging in hobbies or exercise |
| Before making major business decisions | During time set aside for relaxation |
| When discussing with advisors or mentors | In moments of rest or mindfulness |
| While evaluating contingency triggers | When spending time with loved ones |
| During your designated "work block" hours | In your designated "off hours" |
| When updating your team on goals | When recharging or sleeping |
| While setting or adjusting priorities | In moments of self-care |
