deeply held beliefs about money from childhood, often subconscious

## 4 MONEY TYPES

### OPTIMIZER
Wants to plan, track and optimize every possible thing. big on spreadsheets and automation.
Example behavior: very cautious about spending, to the point where they never spend at all and live very cheaply now with the goal of living rich in the future.

### AVOIDER
Uses a series of conscious + unconscious techniques to avoid money.
Example Behavior: doesn't like to open bills and ignores emails about money

### WORRIER
Constantly worries about money, most likely stemming from an environment of scarcity as a kid (saw their parents worry about money)
Example Behavior: Constantly asks things like, "am i going to have enough? am i behind? is it too late?"

### DREAMER
Believes that their fortune is just around the corner - the next gig, the next sale, etc., will make them rich
Example Behavior: struggles with long-term money advice, instead opting for scams/schemes with a get-rich-quick promise.

## ADVICE:

1. **THE AVOIDER**
- look at your spending habits and write them down. what did i spend for the last month?
- focus on small, steady changes to build good financial habits

2. **THE WORRIER**
- when you talk about money, what do you say? how do you feel?
- when i talk about money in the future, how do I want to talk about it?
- when i look at money, how do i want to feel?

3. **THE OPTIMIZER**
- acknowledge that you have already won the game you're trying to play. your setup is already automated and it's going to happen.
- look inside and ask yourself "what do i love and how do i want to spend my money?"

4. **THE DREAMER**
- write down all the gigs and deals you're working on
- objectively rank your income streams, and determine which is the most financially beneficial
- focus on excelling at the income source that is making the most money

### Finding Your Net Worth
1. Assets
2. Investments
3. Savings
4. Debt

- assets: car, house, any property, or business you may own
- investments: any retirement and non-retirement accounts
- savings: checking and savings accounts
- debt: loans, credit card debt, mortgage

Net Worth Formula:
assets + investments + savings - debt = ?

### Conscious Spending Plan
1. **Fixed Cost: 50-60%**
   - mortgage / rent
   - utilities
   - car payment
   - debt
   - groceries (what you need to live life on a monthly basis)
   - this should be 50-60% of your take home pay. or money after taxes

2. **Savings: 5-10%**
   - emergency fund
   - future down payment for a car or house

3. **Investments: 5-10% (this is where wealth is created)**
   - 401K
   - Roth IRA
   - or any other type of investment account that i have
   - 5-10% is the suggestion, but the higher the better

4. **Guilt-Free Spending: 20-35%**
   - Travel
   - Eating Out
   - Clothes
   - all the things you love to do

Think of this like a puzzle. as long as all the pieces are fitting, you can spend money however you want. it's your rich life, not mine.

# Earn More, Stress Less
Why cut costs when you can grow income? Ramit teaches you to identify your marketable skills, find paying clients, and scale your earnings. Transform from a penny-pinching to a profit-building mindset.

> most money advice is focused on cutting and cutting and cutting costs.
> I don't like that.
> Yes, you should understand your spending, and yes, you should stop paying for things you do not value. But if the entire strategy is just "cut more," it turns money into a shrinking game that feels restrictive, exhausting, and small. It also assumes you can win simply by tightening your belt forever, which is not realistic for most people.
> I would rather **focus on growing the amount of money i can make.** Growing income gives you options. It gives you margin. It lets you solve problems with creativity instead of deprivation. And it changes the emotional posture from fear to agency. When you earn more, you can still spend intentionally, but now your plan is built around building skills, increasing value, finding better opportunities, and making smart moves that actually expand your life over time.

## **Four Revenue Accelerators**
1. **Raise your rates**
   1. start high, raise often
   2. if demand increases, prices must follow
2. **Add tiers to the service offering**
   1. offer basic, premium, and VIP versions of your offer
3. **Expand your clients**
   1. look beyond friends — use referrals and platforms
4. **Recurring revenue system**
   1. monthly retainers
   2. subscription
   3. digital product bundles
