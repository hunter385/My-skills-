# Daily

### Morning TAME
1. **Tidy** up our record by updating entries from the previous day, adding any missing information
2. **Acknowledge** whether our actions align with our intentions
3. **Migrate** relevant entries into your new Daily Log. Migrate only the relevant actions for today
4. **Enact** - select your one priority for the day

### Evening TAME
1. **Tidy** the record by updating your action bullets, adding in anything missing, crossing out what is no longer relevant
2. **Acknowledge** the day gone by. How did you move toward or away from your intentions today? What were my wins, progress, or lessons learned?
3. **Migrate** things to their homes - for example out to digital apps. Logging things first before putting them straight into an app can give you an important moment of pause to consider their importance.
4. **Enact** - Do what you need to properly shut down for the day. Rapid log final thoughts or set up the next day's log

# Weekly TAME
1. **Tidy** our records from the past week
2. **Acknowledge** the experience from the week gone by in the Written Reflection (see below)
3. **Migrate** what matters for the next week into your Weekly Action Plan. *Remember that rewriting is important part of the process - if it's not worth rewriting, it not be worth your time.*
4. **Enact** your insight. Rank your top 3 actions for the week.

### Acknowledging Our Intentions
In the Weekly Ritual, we take the time to reflect by Acknowledging how we moved toward or away from our intentions or the life we want to live.

1. As you review your records, identify 1-3 moments, of your own actions, that brought you **Toward** and **Away** from your Intention.
2. Turn to a new spread to create your Weekly Log. The left page will be your Weekly Reflection. Split this page in half. In the top half, you'll process 1-3 **Towards** events. In the bottom half, you'll process 1-3 **Away** events.
3. For each event, follow the following format:
   - o Acknowledge the event
   - = Acknowledge how you felt
   - – Acknowledge what you learned and any insights you had
   - - Acknowledge your agency. Identify one small action to take this week to cultivate what worked or clean up what didn't.

If this is brand new to you, start with 1 of each kind of move. This brevity helps keep this practice approachable and sustainable over time.

*Remember: Away moves doesn't mean there's something wrong with you! You can either beat yourself up (unproductive) or you can take responsibility for your choices and do something differently.*

This is how we begin to write a better life, and get off autopilot.

# Monthly TAME
1. **Tidy** - Clean up your records. Update the previous month's timeline. Notice moments you moved toward or away from your intentions.
2. **Acknowledge** - On a new spread, create your Monthly Reflection. On the left page, reflect on 3 actions that brought you away from your intention. On the right page, reflect on 3 actions that brought you toward your intention. Use the four-bullet format. Again, this is not about judgment, but about noticing what actually happened in our lives with compassion and curiosity so we can better refine and respond.
3. **Migrate** - On a new spread, create your new Monthly Log. On the left, create the **Monthly Timeline** which will log notable events throughout the month. On the right page, create and curate your **Monthly Action Plan** with actions relevant for your upcoming month.
4. **Enact** - Number your top 4 important actions for the following month.

# migration filter
If you answer "yes" to 3 or 4 of these questions, migrate the action. If you say yes to fewer than 3, take time to consider where this action should go. This is what Greg McKeown calls **addition by subtraction** - removing distractions so what remains can be more impactful.

# Future log
### Creating the Log
It's time to go back to the pages you set aside after your index. Since most people use about 2 notebooks a year we create a 6-month Future Log by drawing two lines across and labeling the cells beginning with the month after this one. That's it!

### Curating Your Future
The Future Log is populated in two ways:
1. Write directly into Future Log
2. Capture first, migrate later

# someday log
### About the Someday Log
Sometimes, things we want to do don't have a deadline - like a home project, a hobby you want to try, new initiatives at work. They aren't urgent, but they still matter somehow.

**Someday Log**: Dedicated space to capture ideas, goals, responsibilities that aren't active yet, but are worth holding onto.

This is a living inventory of things that you want to pursue - some are practical, some are fun, but all are possibilities that await you. This is also a great complement to those who use a Digital Calendar.

### Set Up Your Someday Log
Place it anywhere you want, but it works well near the Future Log in the front of your notebook. Simply label the spread "Someday Log," and create a list. Capture these ideas before it disappears. Feel free to add the remaining items from your Mental Inventory - as long as they still matter to you.

### Using Your Someday Log
Once you've created this list, you can pull it into your logs at any point where you have time, resources, and desire. Simply reference it during your Rituals.

# Index
The address book of all of our collections. A birds-eye view of what we're paying attention to in this chapter of our lives.

**Side note**: No need to index your Daily Logs - they're a catchall, so any useful references can be migrated to the right place and Indexed accordingly from there. It can get pretty messy if we index every Daily!

# ending a book
1. **Tidy**. Read the notebook. Tidy up your logs, update the index and future logs.
2. **Acknowledge**. To reflect, we'll do two exercises.
   First, reflect on what actions moved you toward or away from your intentions from this chapter. You can draw from what is still relevant from Weekly and Monthly Rituals. Follow the same process:
   - o Acknowledge what happened
   - = Acknowledge how you felt
   - – Acknowledge what you learned
   - - Acknowledge your agency for the next chapter of your life
   Second, reflect on Process and Purpose. Divide a spread into four cells. On the left, note 1) What worked and 2) What didn't work. On the right, note 1) what you want more of, or 2) what you want less of.
3. **Migrate & Enact**. Time to set up your new notebook and Migrate what matters. Set your intention - who do you want to commit to being in this next chapter of your life?
