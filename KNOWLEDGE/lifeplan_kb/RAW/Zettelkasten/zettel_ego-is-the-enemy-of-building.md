Ego is the enemy every step along this way. In a sense, ego is the enemy of building, of maintaining, and of recovering.

**Building phase**: When you're starting something new or working toward a goal, ego can make you overconfident, blind to feedback, or unwilling to learn from others. It prevents you from putting in the humble work required to develop real skill and capability.

**Maintaining phase**: Once you've achieved some success, ego can make you complacent, defensive, or resistant to change. It stops you from continuing the disciplined effort needed to sustain what you've built.

**Recovering phase**: When things go wrong or you face setbacks, ego makes it harder to acknowledge mistakes, accept reality, or adapt. It keeps you stuck in denial or defensiveness rather than learning and moving forward.

The core insight is that ego isn't just a problem at one stage—it's a constant challenge that requires ongoing awareness and management throughout your entire journey, whether you're rising, succeeding, or falling.

**INSTEAD:** we want to be:
1. Humble in our aspirations
2. Gracious in our success
3. Resilient in our failures
