> why do i feel the pull that my year would be better if i became richer and pursued creating more businesses that align with the b quadrant of rich dad poor dad?

---

Hunter, this is a wise question. And it deserves a careful, compassionate answer.

You are not wrong for feeling this pull. But the pull needs to be interpreted, not obeyed blindly. Let us slow it down.

## **First, name the desire without judging it**
Desire itself is not the problem. Desire is information.
The longing to build wealth, create businesses, and move into the B quadrant is not inherently unspiritual. Scripture is full of builders, stewards, landowners, and creators of enterprise.

What matters is **what the desire is trying to give you**. Desire is always reaching for something deeper.

## **What this pull is really pointing toward**

### **1. A longing for agency and stewardship**
You are wired to build. The B quadrant represents ownership, leverage, and long term structures. That speaks to your Builder and Visionary identity. You want to steward ideas in a way that multiplies impact beyond your personal effort. That is not greed. That is vocation.

In Genesis, God gives humans the call to cultivate, multiply, and steward creation. Business can be one expression of that calling.

### **2. A longing for freedom, not luxury**
When you imagine being richer, I do not hear fantasies of excess. I hear a desire for margin. Margin to choose. Margin to rest. Margin to say no. Margin to focus on what matters most.

Money promises relief from constraint. That makes sense. The danger is not wanting margin. The danger is believing money alone can provide it.

### **3. A longing for security in an uncertain world**
Leadership carries risk. Responsibility for family awakens fear, even when we do not name it. The B quadrant offers the illusion of safety. Systems instead of dependence. Assets instead of vulnerability.

Jesus names this gently. "You cannot serve both God and money." Not because money is evil, but because it makes a convincing substitute for trust.

### **4. A longing for legacy and generational impact**
You think in decades, not weeks. You want to leave something your kids can stand on. Something transferable. Something that outlives you.

Business ownership feels like a tangible way to do that. This is a good longing. But legacy without formation becomes hollow. Legacy with formation becomes a blessing.

## **Why it feels especially strong right now**
This pull intensifies in seasons when:
- you feel spiritually restless
- your ambition is waking up
- you are confronting limitation or dependence
- you are tasting clarity and wondering what more is possible

In other words, this pull is surfacing because you are growing. The question is not, should I pursue this or suppress it? The better question is, **what does this desire need to be healed, ordered, and purified by God**?

## **The spiritual risk to name honestly**
Here is the gentle warning. If wealth and business are pursued as **relief** from anxiety, insecurity, or restlessness, they will never satisfy you. You already know this pattern. No amount of money can fill the God sized ache you named.

But if wealth and business are pursued as **stewardship**, offered back to God, held with open hands, they can become a means of love, generosity, and freedom. The difference is not the activity. It is the attachment.

## **Jesus does not ask you to kill ambition**
He asks you to **submit it**. Seek first the kingdom of God. That does not mean stop building. It means let God decide the order, pace, and purpose of your building.

Ambition baptized becomes calling. Ambition unsubmitted becomes anxiety.

## **A practice for discernment**
Instead of asking, "Should I pursue this?"

Ask this in prayer for the next season. "Lord, what am I hoping money and business will give me that I am not receiving from You right now?"

Sit with that question slowly. God often meets us underneath our ambition, not against it.

## **A question to carry**
If God gave you financial abundance, what kind of man would you need to become to carry it without losing your soul?

That is the formation question beneath the business question. And it is the right one.

You are not wrong to feel this pull. You are being invited to let God shape it. We will keep discerning this together.
