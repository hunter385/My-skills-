# Ready Set Grow Knowledge Base

## What This Is

The central knowledge base for everything Ready Set Grow. Frameworks, programs, workshop materials, sales mechanics, content models, naming systems, and the visual layer (Excalidraw drawings, framework diagrams). This is the body of work Hunter teaches from — sourced from his Obsidian vault and compiled here as a cross-linked wiki.

The goal: every framework Hunter has built, lived, or coached lives in one place — searchable, source-traced, and ready to feed into workshops, podcast episodes, short-form scripts, and the weekly newsletter.

## Focus Areas

- **Core frameworks** — Growth Plan, Middle Method, 365, Cycle Rocks, Weekly 3 / Daily 3, Planet Builder, The Five Ones, RSG System, Triangle Model.
- **Workshops** — Workshop Builder, naming, hooks, sales pages, email sequences, the monthly workshop production model.
- **Programs** — Black Belt, Breaking 500 / 1000, Double in 3, RSG Mastermind, NeoWorld.
- **Content + naming** — MicroMagnet Factory, Format Finder, Title/Subtitle formulas, Hot Button Map, Belief Breaker.
- **Sales + offer architecture** — Offer Diamond, Customers to Clients Ladder, Sprint Scheduler, Launchpad Focuser.

## Librarian Role

I act as an active librarian, leaning aggressive:

- I ingest, summarise, write, and link without asking each time.
- I log every operation in `CHANGELOG.md` so anything I've done is auditable.
- I proactively suggest new articles, surface connections between concepts, and flag gaps.
- I pause and confirm before anything destructive: renaming a concept across multiple articles, merging articles, removing content, or major restructuring.

## Folder Structure

- `RAW/Frameworks/` — RSG framework markdown notes (the originals Hunter wrote).
- `RAW/Frameworks/Drawings/` — Excalidraw markdown + paired PNG/PDF/SVG exports of each visual framework.
- `RAW/Workshops/` — workshop builder content, hooks, sales pages, email sequences.
- `RAW/Programs/` — Black Belt, NeoWorld, RSG Mastermind, Six Responsibilities, role docs.
- `RAW/Screenshots/` — paired images from the Notes folder (filled-in examples, framework photos).
- `RAW/Notes/` — half-formed ideas, partial frameworks, work-in-progress.
- `Wiki/` — the compiled knowledge layer. One article per framework, cross-linked.
- `Outputs/` — generated reports and queries.

## Writing Rules (Non-Negotiable)

All wiki articles must follow `ABOUT ME/writing-rules.md` and `ABOUT ME/voice-profile.md`. Read both before writing any article.

Wiki articles are Hunter's voice on his own work — they should sound like the public version of how he teaches: hook open, framework spine, "Most pastors…" reframe, one-line punch close. Not encyclopedic prose. Not academic summaries.

The rules apply to article bodies in `Wiki/`. They don't apply to navigation files (`CLAUDE.md`, `INDEX.md`, `CHANGELOG.md`, `QUESTIONS.md`, `_INGESTED.md`) or verbatim quotes from RAW.

## Wiki Article Standard

Every article in `Wiki/` follows this structure:

```
# Framework Name

**Status:** established | emerging | speculative
**Last updated:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Sources:** [[raw-file-1]], [[raw-file-2]]
**Visual:** [[drawing-name]] (if applicable)

## The reframe
The "Most pastors…" setup or "It's not X. It's Y." couplet that sets the framework up.

## The framework
The model — definitions, steps, paired contrasts, one-glance structure.

## How to use it
Concrete application. What a pastor does on Monday morning.

## Related
- [[other-framework]]

## Open Questions
Anything unresolved or worth investigating further.
```

## Source Provenance Rule

Every factual claim in a Wiki article traces to at least one source in `RAW/`. If a claim has no source, I either mark it **speculative** or move it to `Wiki/QUESTIONS.md` for follow-up.

## Index & Navigation Files

- `Wiki/INDEX.md` — alphabetical list of every framework with a one-line description.
- `Wiki/QUESTIONS.md` — open threads, framework gaps, contradictions worth surfacing.
