## The reframe

Momentum fades. Not because the vision was wrong. Not because people stopped caring. Because the team doesn't have a system built for the middle. The middle is where teams break. Most leadership content is built for the start and the end. Almost none of it is built for the middle.

## The framework

The Middle Method installs an execution rhythm that protects teams from drift, urgency-hijack, and new-idea disruption mid-cycle.

**Two units make up the rhythm:**

- **Cycle** — a focused execution sprint. Picks the few priorities that matter. Holds them protected. Cycles fight distraction.
- **Cooldown** — a reflection and reset period at the end of each Cycle. Reviews what happened. Decides what's next. Cooldowns fight misalignment.

Cycles without Cooldowns lead to burnout.
Cooldowns without Cycles lead to stagnation.
Run both.

### Cycle length is calibrated to the business, not fixed

The Middle Method is **cadence-agnostic**. The principle is fixed (Cycles + Cooldowns, in rhythm). The duration scales with the speed of the business.

| Context | Cycle | Cooldown |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **What RSG teaches churches** | 90 days | 30 days |
| **Ready Set Grow (Hunter's own business)** | 6 weeks | ~1 week |
| **NeoWorld (Hunter's COO business)** | Monthly | Embedded in the month |

Why different cadences:

- **Churches** need 90-day cycles. Cultural change inside a church team is slow. Shorter cycles don't give a Cycle Rock time to land before the team tries to reset it.
- **RSG-the-business** runs on 6-week cycles. It's a content business with weekly publishing rhythm — needs faster iteration than a church team.
- **NeoWorld** runs monthly. Streaming, inventory, and Whatnot platform dynamics turn over fast — a 90-day cycle would be three lifetimes of market shift.

**The rule:** Pick the longest cycle length that still allows the team to iterate at the speed of its market. Then never change it mid-Cycle.

## How to use it

**Week 0 (start of Cycle):**

- Confirm the 3 Cycle Rocks (the priorities that matter for the next 90 days).
- Confirm 365 role clarity for everyone on the team (3 Cycle Goals, 6 Core Responsibilities, 5 Team Values).
- Lock the calendar. No new ideas added mid-cycle.

**Weeks 1–12 (running the Cycle):**

- Weekly 3 — what gets done this week to move Cycle Rocks forward.
- Daily 3 — what gets done today to move Weekly 3 forward.
- No mid-cycle priority swaps. If a new urgent idea comes in, capture it for the next Cycle planning meeting. Don't dump it on the team now.

**Weeks 13–16 (Cooldown):**

- Reflect on what worked, what didn't, what got dropped.
- Reset priorities for the next Cycle.
- Re-align team on what comes next.
- Then start the next Cycle.

## Why the middle is the real enemy

Most teams have plenty of vision. They have plenty of ideas. What they don't have is a system that holds focus long enough for important work to compound. Without a Middle Method, three things happen between week 4 and week 8:

1. **Urgency hijacks priorities.** Something feels urgent, the team pivots, the Cycle Rock drifts.
2. **New ideas get introduced mid-cycle.** Leader gets excited, dumps it on the team, momentum collapses.
3. **Role clarity decays.** Team members start defining success differently than the leader. The "Newlywed Game Problem."

The Middle Method is the structural answer to all three.
