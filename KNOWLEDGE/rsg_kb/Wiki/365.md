## The reframe

Most pastors think their team is the problem. They're not. Your team is mirroring your lack of clarity.

Job descriptions fail because they describe the role in too much detail and too little direction. People walk away with a list of duties but no scorecard for the cycle. 365 solves the "Newlywed Game Problem" — when team members assume alignment but define success differently than the leader.

## The framework

For every role on the team, 365 specifies:

- **3 Cycle Goals** — what success in this role looks like for the current 90-day Cycle.
- **6 Core Responsibilities** — what you are always accountable for, every Cycle.
- **5 Team Values** — the cultural standards that govern how you operate.

That's 3 + 6 + 5 = 14 lines of role clarity. Fits on an index card. Memorable.

## How to use it

**Build cadence:**

- Build (or refresh) every team member's 365 during the Cooldown that ends each Cycle.
- Cycle Goals reset every Cycle. Core Responsibilities are stable across Cycles. Team Values are stable across years.
- Cycle Goals must trace back to Cycle Rocks (the team-level priorities) and the Growth Plan (the annual direction).

**Operating cadence:**

- Use 365 as the 1:1 framework. "How are you tracking against your 3 Cycle Goals?"
- Use the 6 Core Responsibilities as the accountability lens. If someone is consistently dropping a Core Responsibility, that's a role-fit conversation.
- Use the 5 Team Values as the cultural lens. Behavior outside the values is a culture conversation.

## Why job descriptions fail

A job description lists tasks. 365 picks the war.

- Tasks accumulate. Cycle Goals concentrate.
- Tasks describe activity. Cycle Goals describe outcomes.
- Tasks have no time horizon. Cycle Goals expire every 90 days.

365 is built to be reviewable in under 5 minutes during a 1:1. A job description isn't.

## Hunter's own 365 example

Hunter's 6 Core Responsibilities (from About Me):

1. One live workshop per month.
2. Four podcast episodes per month.
3. Five short-form videos per week.
4. Weekly coaching call segment (alternating weeks).
5. Read and shape the next cycle.
6. CEO duties — strategy, framework creation, execution architecture.

The Cycle Goals reset every 90 days but live underneath these stable responsibilities.
