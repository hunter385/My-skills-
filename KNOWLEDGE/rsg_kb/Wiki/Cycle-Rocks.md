## The reframe

You don't have an idea problem. You have a priority problem.

Most teams enter a 90-day Cycle with 20 things they're going to "focus on." Then nothing finishes, because everything is competing. Cycle Rocks force a choice: 3 priorities for the Cycle. That's it. Everything else is "not this Cycle."

The metaphor is the jar of big rocks (Covey). If you put the small rocks in first, the big rocks don't fit. Cycle Rocks are the big rocks. Pick them first. Schedule them first. Protect them first.

## The framework

A Cycle Rock is:

- A focused priority for the 90-day Cycle.
- Concrete enough to define "done" — not "improve our community presence" but "launch the new welcome team training and run it with 10 cohorts."
- Owned by a specific person (the Rock Owner) — not the team in general.
- Tracked weekly via the Cycle Rock Dashboard.

**Rule of 3:** Pick three Cycle Rocks per Cycle. No more, no fewer.

- One is too few — the Cycle has no breadth.
- Five is too many — the Cycle has no focus.

## How to use it

**Selection (during Cooldown):**

- Review the Growth Plan. What direction does the next Cycle need to push?
- Brainstorm 10–15 candidate Rocks.
- Apply the priority filter: must serve the Growth Plan, must be finishable in 90 days, must have a clear Owner.
- Narrow to 3. Name them with action-first headers (not noun-only topic headers). Example: "Launch the new welcome team training" — not "Welcome team."

**Tracking (during Cycle):**

- Cycle Rock Dashboard: visible to the whole team. Each Rock has a status (Green / Yellow / Red) and a one-line update.
- Updated weekly during the team's Cycle review meeting.
- Each Rock Owner reports their status in 60 seconds.

**Protecting (against drift):**

- New ideas that aren't a Cycle Rock get captured for the next Cooldown's brainstorm. They don't get added mid-Cycle.
- If a Cycle Rock is consistently Red for 3+ weeks, that's a "we need to triage" conversation — not an "add more Rocks" conversation.

## How Rocks roll up and down

```
Growth Plan
   ↓
3 Cycle Rocks (team-level priorities)
   ↓
365 Cycle Goals (each person's 3 Cycle Goals trace back to the Rocks)
   ↓
Weekly 3 (what this week moves a Cycle Goal / Rock forward)
   ↓
Daily 3 (what today moves the Weekly 3 forward)
```

Cycle Rocks are the load-bearing layer between the annual direction and the weekly traction.
