# Six Responsibilities (Hunter's 365)

**Status:** established
**Last updated:** 2026-05-30
**Sources:** [[Six Responsibilities.md]] (Programs), About Me, voice profile

## What this is

Hunter's own 365 — applied to himself as CEO of Ready Set Grow. These are the six things he is accountable for every Cycle. Cycle Goals reset every 90 days; these responsibilities don't.

This article exists in the wiki because (a) it's an example of [[365]] in practice, and (b) it's the operating contract for what Claude should expect from Hunter's content production.

## The six

### 1. One live workshop per month

Design and teach one live workshop each month that gives pastors a practical, implementable framework. Highest-leverage acquisition and retention event each Cycle. Owns the full creation and delivery — teaching outline through room experience.

### 2. Four podcast episodes per month

Batch film four podcast episodes in a single sitting. One clear idea per episode, tied to a current framework or pain point, moving a pastor closer to a decision or breakthrough.

### 3. Five short-form videos per week (Instagram + YouTube)

Five short-form videos weekly that distill one idea, reframe one problem, or introduce one framework concept. Top-of-funnel acquisition. Hunter owns ideation and on-camera delivery; distribution is the team's.

### 4. Prepare and deliver his segment on weekly coaching calls

Alternates with Mark. When it's Hunter's week, he shows up fully prepared — clear framework, focused teaching point, space for breakthrough. When it's Mark's week, he shows up as a present, engaged coach.

### 5. Read and shape the next Cycle

Dedicated weekly time for reading, capturing ideas, and shaping what RSG teaches next. The strategic fuel. What he reads becomes what he teaches. What he notices becomes what RSG solves.

### 6. CEO duties

Strategy. Leadership. Framework creation. Execution architecture. The work no one else can do for him.

## What "winning the Cycle" looks like

If Hunter is executing all six with excellence, RSG is growing. That's the success function for his role.

## How this connects to the rest of the operating system

- **Cycle Goals** layered on top: every Cycle, Hunter picks 3 Cycle Goals that sit underneath these stable responsibilities.
- **Weekly 3** allocates the week's work across the six.
- **Daily 3** allocates the day to the highest-leverage of the six right now.
- **Cooldown:** review whether all six got executed at the right depth, what needs to shift for the next Cycle.

## Reverse-engineered priorities (for the AI to know)

When Hunter asks for help, the request usually maps to one of these six:

| If he asks for help with… | The responsibility is… |
|---|---|
| Workshop topic, design, delivery | Responsibility 1 |
| Podcast episode brief, batch prep | Responsibility 2 |
| Short-form scripts, video ideas | Responsibility 3 |
| Coaching call segment prep | Responsibility 4 |
| Reading capture, framework architecture, idea audit | Responsibility 5 |
| RSG strategy, offer design, team direction | Responsibility 6 |

This mapping is what makes the RSG content skills (`/framework-architect`, `/workshop-builder`, `/podcast-brief`, `/short-form-engine`, `/email-architect`, `/coaching-call-prep`) cleanly assignable.
