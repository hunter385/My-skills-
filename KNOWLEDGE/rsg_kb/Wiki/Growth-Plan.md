## The reframe

A directional statement isn't a mission statement.

Most pastors confuse vision with strategy. They write a mission statement, hang it on the wall, and assume the team is aligned. Then nothing happens. The Growth Plan is the strategic layer between vision and execution — the document that turns "where we want to go" into "what we're actually doing about it."

## The framework

The Growth Plan answers one question: **Where are we going?**

It sits upstream of every other framework in the RSG operating system. Without it, Cycles aim at nothing. Cycle Rocks pick themselves at random. Teams optimize for activity, not direction.

The Growth Plan defines:

- The directional statement (where we're headed)
- The non-negotiables (what we won't sacrifice on the way)
- The strategic priorities for the year (what makes this year different)
- The departmental cascade (how each department contributes)

## How to use it

- Write or rewrite the Growth Plan during an annual Cooldown.
- Build Cycle Rocks downstream of it — every Rock must trace back to the Growth Plan.
- Use it as the audit lens in monthly 1:1s and quarterly reviews. "Does what we're doing this week connect to the Growth Plan?"
- Cascade into Departmental Growth Plans so each department leader has their own version that aligns with the church-wide one.

## Why this is upstream

The whole RSG operating system flows from here:

```
Growth Plan → Middle Method → 365 → Cycle Rocks → Weekly 3 / Daily 3
```

The Growth Plan creates direction. The Middle Method creates rhythm. 365 creates role clarity. Cycle Rocks create focused priorities. Weekly 3 / Daily 3 creates daily traction.

Skip the Growth Plan and the whole system aims at nothing in particular.
