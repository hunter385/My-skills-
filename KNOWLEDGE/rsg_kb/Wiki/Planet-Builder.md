## The reframe

Most teachers improvise structure on the fly. They have a topic. They have some points. They hope it lands. It usually doesn't.

The Planet Builder is Hunter's structural answer to long-form teaching. It's not a manuscript. It's not a script. It's an architecture — four beats, in order, every time. The structure is the discipline. Inside the structure, Hunter teaches from a drawing.

## The framework

Long-form teaching follows four beats:

1. **WHY** — Frustrations + Wants + Mistakes. Build the pain. Surface the audience's frustrations. Articulate what they actually want (which is rarely what they say they want). Name the most common mistakes they're making.

2. **WHAT** — Big Idea + Metaphor + Model. Introduce the reframe. Anchor it with a picture-able physical metaphor. Show the model (the framework itself).

3. **HOW** — Worksheet with steps. Concrete application. What the listener does on Monday morning. The framework becomes a verb.

4. **NOW** — Remember + Actions. Recap what to remember. Name the concrete next action.

## How to use it

**Designing a long-form teaching:**

- Open with a problem statement, principle statement, or provocative claim. No relational warmups. (See [[HOOK-BUILD-PAYOFF-INVITATION]] for the short-form analog.)
- Spend 30–40% of the time on WHY. Pain builds the appetite for the framework.
- Spend 30% on WHAT. The model is the centerpiece — but only if WHY did its job.
- Spend 20–25% on HOW. Action steps, examples, worksheet. This is where the audience graduates from "interesting" to "I'm doing this."
- Close on NOW. Don't summarize. Issue an instruction.

**Teaching from a drawing, not a manuscript:**

- Hunter teaches from the Excalidraw / reMarkable / Keynote visual of the framework, not a written script.
- The Planet Builder beats become a mental sequence the speaker walks through using the visual as the anchor.
- This is why frameworks must be one-glance memorable. If the speaker has to remember the framework, the listener has no shot.

## Why this works

- **WHY before WHAT.** Most teachers jump to the framework too fast. The Planet Builder forces a delay — pain first, model second. Hunter's productive contradiction: "Jumps to the framework too fast, but believes building tension and pain first lands harder." The Planet Builder is the structural answer to that contradiction.

- **Triads + a fourth beat.** WHY / WHAT / HOW is a triad. NOW is the close. Four beats is the max before listeners lose the structure. The Planet Builder lives at the structural edge.

- **A worksheet, not a feeling.** HOW must produce a concrete worksheet step. Not "be more intentional." Not "lean in." A specific move. This is where teaching becomes coaching.
