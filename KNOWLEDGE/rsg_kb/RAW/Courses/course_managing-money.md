# ATTENTION DIRECTING

**3 IMPORTANT FINANCIAL STATEMENTS** — *HOW THE HELL ARE WE DOING?*
- **Income Statement** — Profit and loss. All income. All expenses. Hopefully, what's left... Profit.
- **Balance Sheet** — Snapshot of any particular day on the state of the business. Assets | Liabilities | Net Worth (or retained Profit).
- **Cash Flow** — Summarizes the amount of cash and cash equivalents entering and leaving a company.

**GIVING NUMBERS MEANING**
Numbers don't have meaning outside of comparison. $40,000 profit is amazing if your goal was $10k, fine if $40k, bad if $60k.

**BALANCE SHEET** — two numbers to look at:
- Accounts Receivable: money due to a firm for goods/services delivered but not yet paid.
- Accounts Payable: short-term debt the company owes its creditors.

Accounts Receivable should be 2:1 bigger than Accounts Payable ("working capital").

**CASH FLOW**
A cash flow statement is the truest sense of how much money you have, because it includes all the variables.
