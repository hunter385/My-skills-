# Question
[QUESTION] what is the best practice to record weeklies and dailies. We are using Google Sheets, ....it's meh. Besides basecamp, what is everyone using?


# Answer

Hi Patrick!  
  
### APPROACH 1  
  
Obviously you know we use basecamp. If you're finding that Google Sheets isn't working, I would ask, what are you and your team using for communication? That is the place I would recommend looking at first.  
  
I've found multiple teams using  
- a group text  
- slack  
- group me  
  
for the weekly 3 daily 3 and finding a lot of success in it. The only hard part about this is there is nothing automated to make this happen every day. You have to remember to send out something like:  
  
“Drop your Daily 3 👇 What matters most today?”  
“Weekly 3 time! What 3 wins or focuses will move you closest to your cycle rocks this week?”


### APPROACH 2

If your team isn’t consistently filling out their Daily 3 and Weekly 3, you might not have to solve it with a better tool. You could instead look into fixing it with an accountability loop.

Add a standing agenda item to every manager's 1:1 meetings:  
“Review my direct report’s Daily 3 and Weekly 3.”

This does two things:
- 1. Makes the work visible
- 2. Creates a weekly moment of accountability

When people know their work will be reviewed, consistency goes up.

#### The principle

People don’t always do what’s expected.  
They do what’s inspected.

#### What to do next

- Add this as a recurring header in every 1:1
- Train managers to actually review their work, not skim their work
- Tie it to performance conversations

That’s how you could possible turn “meh tracking” into a system that drives execution.
