## Core Idea
There's two paths you can take here: 
<mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Path A</mark>
<mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">Path B</mark>



**Path A** tries to convert *leads* into *clients* right away
**Path B** gets *leads* to first become *customers* then asks for *clients*



<mark style="background: #FF5582A6;"> pay zero for my webinar--> pay high ticket</mark>
This is what people try to do with the webinar route—pay nothing, then pitch high-ticket offers.


<mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">pay zero --> pay something for my workshop --> pay high ticket</mark>
It's easier to convert leads into customers at a low price point first, then into clients afterward.


**LEADS → CUSTOMERS → SALES:** 
Instead of trying to convince strangers to buy expensive programs right away, first sell them something small and useful (like a $100 workshop). Once they've paid you and gotten results, it's much easier to invite them to work with you at a higher level. Think of it as building trust through a small transaction before asking for a big commitment.


## When To Use
- trying to warm up leads in marketing
- when designing how we will fill up our product with clients
