# Question 
How often do you allow staff to utilize ‘on the clock’ time for activities like golf?   

  have a staff person who has recently been leaving the office a couple of hours early 1-2 times per week to play golf. Sometimes with others in the ministry as connection. Sometimes just having fun.  
  
I think sometimes he takes 1-2 guys with him to play and uses it as a way to get out of the office, which I’m not completely opposed to, but I also feel like he would rather go do that than take some of that time to work on growing spiritually and personally.  
  
Not sure what’s typically appropriate for this type of thing.


---

# Idea

“I want you to be at the office 9-5 vs I don’t care where you are if you’re getting your stuff done” talk


[[3. Resources/Excalidraw/9–5 vs. Results]]





## 🧩 The Framework — Planet Builder Filled

**NOW (What's real right now)**

**Remember:** Staff culture is set by what you allow, not what you say. Right now you're allowing ambiguity. Every staff member is watching how you handle this.

**Actions:** You've been observing but not addressing. That gap creates resentment or entitlement — either in you or in him.

**Next:** You need to define your operating culture for _everyone_, then have a 1:1 with this person from that framework — not as a reaction to the golf.

**Frustrations:** He's leaving early 1-2x/week. You're not sure it's justified. You feel like he'd rather golf than grow. You sense a motivation/priority misalignment.

**Wants:** You want staff who are self-motivated to grow, not just self-motivated to have fun. You want presence and output. You want a culture where work ethic isn't policed but owned.

**Mistakes:** Not defining expectations upfront. Watching this go on without clarity. Conflating leisure with laziness without knowing if his work is actually suffering.

---

**HOW (Execution layer)**

**Worksheet:** The real conversation is a culture-setting moment. Build it around three questions:

1. What does winning in your role look like this cycle? (365)
2. What does our team's time culture expect of everyone?
3. Where are you investing in your own growth right now?

**Big Idea/Metaphor:** The leader who doesn't define the fence can't complain when the team plays near the edge.

**Model:** Results-first culture with clear rhythms. Not clock-in/clock-out. But not "do whatever" either. It's: _here's what winning looks like, here's what our team rhythm requires, here's where you have freedom._

**WHY** → Staff freedom only works when accountability is explicit and outcomes are clear.

**WHAT** → A team time/culture policy + updated 365 for this person + a direct 1:1 conversation.

---

## 🚀 What To Do

**Step 1 — Decide your actual culture** before the conversation. Choose one:

- Results-based: freedom when outcomes are met
- Presence-based: expected hours with flexibility built in
- Hybrid: core hours + output requirements

**Step 2 — Build a simple team agreement** (1 page max). What does your team expect of each other in terms of presence, availability, and personal growth investment? This protects you from this being a personal attack on him.

**Step 3 — Have two separate conversations:**

- _Conversation A:_ "Here's our team culture and what I expect." (Standard-setting — for everyone.)
- _Conversation B:_ "I want to talk about your personal growth investment." (Developmental — specific to him.)

**Step 4 — Get him current on his 365.** If his 3 cycle goals and 6 responsibilities are clear, you have an objective metric. Is the golf interfering with those? That's your accountability anchor — not the hours.
