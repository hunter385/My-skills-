🧠 Build-to-Exit: Top 10 Rules

  

  

  

1) Build a business, not a job

  

  

If it needs you daily, it’s not sellable.

Exit test: Can it run without you for 30–60 days?

  

  

  

  

2) Predictability > growth spikes

  

  

Buyers pay for consistent, repeatable revenue, not volatility.

  

- Recurring > one-time
- Stable > explosive

  

  

  

  

  

3) Kill key-man risk

  

  

If you are the secret sauce, you are the risk.

Replace:

  

- You → systems
- You → trained operators

  

  

  

  

  

4) Systemize everything

  

  

If it’s not written, it doesn’t exist.

  

- SOPs
- Playbooks
- Training docs

  

  

Goal: A stranger can run it.

  

  

  

  

5) Focus on retention, not just acquisition

  

  

High churn kills valuation.

Simple truth:

More retention = higher multiples

  

  

  

  

6) Own a niche

  

  

General businesses are harder to sell.

Specific = valuable

  

- Clear ICP
- Clear problem
- Clear outcome

  

  

  

  

  

7) Clean up your financials

  

  

Messy books = lower trust = lower price

You need:

  

- Clear P&L
- Consistent margins
- No “mystery expenses”

  

  

  

  

  

8) Build a leadership layer

  

  

A sellable business has:

  

- Managers
- Decision-makers
- Accountability structure

  

  

Not just doers.

  

  

  

  

9) Reduce risk everywhere

  

  

Buyers discount uncertainty:

  

- Customer concentration
- Platform dependence
- Revenue swings

  

  

Your job: remove scary things

  

  

  

  

10) Make it transferable

  

  

The ultimate question:

  

“Can someone else step in and win quickly?”

  

If not, it’s not ready.

  

  

  

  

⚡ Bottom line

  

  

You don’t get paid for revenue.

You get paid for certainty.
