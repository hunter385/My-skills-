# The Framework
---
1. **Outcomes**  
    Get crystal clear on what you actually want and why.
2. **Options**  
    Generate multiple paths. Robbins says:
    - 1 option = no choice
    - 2 options = dilemma
    - 3+ options = real choice
3. **Consequences**  
    List the upsides, downsides, costs, and tradeoffs of each option.
4. **Evaluate**  
    Assess probability, emotional impact, and alignment with your priorities/values.
5. **Mitigate**  
    Reduce downside risk. Ask: “How do I make the bad outcomes less damaging?”
6. **Resolve**  
    Commit fully once the best option is clear. No half-in/half-out decision making.






## **O**utcome: 
- what do i want? 
- Put the outcomes in triage order. 
- then you put the why 
- make sure you're not writing out what do i fear

## **O**ptions: 
- write out all the options on how i could get there
- even the options that are on the table that I don't like

## **C**onsequences: 
- what is the upside and downsides to each of these decisions to the best of my knowledge?


## **E**valuate: 
- what are the probabilities of those consequences?
- is there a slim positive chance of this great result happening?
- is there a slim negative chance that that negative result ends up happening?
- don't let the extremes persuade or dissuade you

## **M**itigation: 
- is there anything i can do to mitigate the possible downsides?

## **R**esolve: 
- Commit fully once the best option is clear. No half-in/half-out decision making.


## Core Idea
don't do this in your head. do it on paper.
