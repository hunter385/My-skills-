### Customer Segments
The specific group of people you're building for. Not everyone — a defined group with shared problems, shared context, and shared goals.

Key question: Who exactly are you serving?

Demographics
Psychographics
Stage / size


### Value Proposition
The specific problem you solve and the result you create for your customer. This is the core reason someone chooses you over doing nothing or choosing someone else.

Key question: Why does someone pay you money?

Outcome
Pain relief
Transformation


### Channels
The paths through which customers find you, experience your value, and buy from you. Channels are how your value proposition reaches your customer segment.

Key question: How do customers discover, evaluate, and buy from you?

Discovery
Conversion
Delivery


### Customer Relationships
How you interact with your customers — how you acquire them, keep them, and grow them. This is about the experience they have with you over time.

Key question: How do you get, keep, and grow your customers?

Acquisition
Retention
Upsell / ascend


### Key Activities
The core things your business must actually DO to deliver its value. These are the non-negotiable actions that create, deliver, and sustain your offer.

Key question: What must you do consistently to deliver results?

Creating
Teaching
Delivering
Selling


### Key Resources
The critical assets your business needs to function — what you own, know, or have access to. This includes people, IP, platforms, and proprietary frameworks.

Key question: What do you own or control that makes the business work?

IP & frameworks
People
Content library
Platform & tools


### Key Partners
The people, organizations, or companies you rely on to make your model work — but that you don't own or fully control. These relationships let you do what you do without having to build everything yourself.

Key question: Who do you depend on?

Vendors
Strategic allies
Distribution partners
Platform providers


### Cost Structure
All the real costs of running the business — what it actually takes to operate. This includes fixed costs (always there) and variable costs (scale with activity).

Key question: What does it cost to run this?

Fixed costs
Variable costs
Team
Tools & platforms


### Revenue Streams
The different ways your business makes money. Each stream is a distinct way a customer pays you for a specific piece of value.

Key question: How do you make money, and from what?

Recurring
One-time
Event-based
