# Question
The values you use in the all staff meeting seem to change based on the season the team is in. Do you recommend that for church teams as well? If so, how do you evaluate team members during their 1:1's?



# RESPONSE

## **Short Answer**
Yes — I believe your staff values should shift with the season.  
But evaluation should stay consistent.
It's just evaluation based on the new set of characteristics we've set before the cycle started.


## **1. Should values change by season?**

**Yes. 100%.**

Here’s why:

- if your **vision** changes yearly on what goals you're pursuing
- Your **values** should flex to support the characteristics that are needed

Think of values like **“what we need most right now to win.”**

Examples:

- Growth season → simplify, evangelize, focus on what matters most
- Stabilization season → systems, consistency, follow-through
- Turnaround season → honesty, ownership, grit

This is aligned with how values actually work:

Values are the characteristics of how we must behave to achieve the vision.  

## **Bottom line**

- Yes → change values with the season (i would suggest no more than once or twice per year)
- No → don’t change how you evaluate people

You always evaluate:
Results (goals) + Ownership (responsibilities) + Behavior (values)


**Values shift to drive the season.** 
**Evaluation stays fixed to drive accountability.**
