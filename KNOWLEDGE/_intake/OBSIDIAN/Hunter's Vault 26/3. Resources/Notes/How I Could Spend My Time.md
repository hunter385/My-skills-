# FUTURE VISION

🔭 **Core:** Your brain naturally lives in tomorrow.
You instinctively imagine possibilities, systems, and future outcomes.

#### ❓Questions to Ask Myself
- Weekly: What future am I building?
- Quarterly: What will exist in 3 years because of my work today?
- Vision question: If everything worked perfectly, what would this become?

#### 🕓 How to Spend My Time
🧠 I need deep work **thinking space**. This is not optional.

| Visionary thinking requires | Without this time you feel |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| whiteboard time                 | reactive                       |
| writing                         | overwhelmed                    |
| thinking walks                  | scattered                      |
| long-form reading               |                                |
| ideation                        |                                |

🔒 **Design rule:** Protect Vision Time. Example rhythm: 2–3 hours weekly, no phone, whiteboard thinking.

# IDEAS + DISCERNMENT

💡 **Working Genius pairing**: Invention + Discernment You generate ideas fast, then evaluate what is real and worth building.


#### ❓Questions to Ask Myself
**Idea questions**
- What problem needs solving?
- What system could fix this?

**Discernment questions**
- Is this actually a good idea?
- Will this work in reality?

🧪 **Filter question:** Is this worth pursuing for 10 years?

#### 🕓 How to Spend My Time
**Best work**
- designing frameworks
- creating courses
- building systems
- strategy sessions
- writing ideas

**Worst work**
- repetitive operations
- detailed administration
- maintenance work

🗃️ **Design rule:** Capture ideas constantly. Keep an idea notebook, framework journal, or voice notes.

# The Biggest Risks for Me

|**Risk**|**What it looks like**|**Early signal**|
|---|---|---|
|Overworking|You keep pushing past the point of diminishing returns. You say yes to “one more thing” and rest becomes optional.|Sleep gets lighter. Irritability rises. You feel guilty when you stop.|
|Starting too many things|New ideas feel urgent, so you spin up projects before the last one is finished. Your attention gets fragmented.|You have lots of motion, but fewer clear wins. Open loops multiply.|
|Measuring yourself by output|Your worth starts riding on production. If you are not shipping, you feel behind, even if you are doing the right work.|A “good day” equals volume. A “slow day” feels like failure.|
|Always chasing the next goal|As soon as you hit a milestone, you move the goalposts. You do not let yourself enjoy completion or gratitude.|You cannot name a recent win you actually celebrated.|
