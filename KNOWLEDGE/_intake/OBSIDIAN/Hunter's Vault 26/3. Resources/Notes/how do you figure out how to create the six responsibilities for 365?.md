# Answer
Hey Paul!

Here’s how I like to think about it:
  
# QUESTIONS I’D USE TO HELP ME FILTER

- “If this role is crushing it… what is measurably better?”

- "if we were having dinner, and I asked you how your youth pastor (or any role on your team) was doing, and you said they were KILLING IT! What are they doing well in from your perspective?"

# HOW TO GET TO THE 6

- 1. Brain dump everything that comes to mind on how you think they should invest their time
- 2. Circle the 6 most important (force yourself to choose)
- 3. Clean them up into clear ownership statements

# WAYS TO BUILD THEM

- 1. You draft them → share with them

- 2. They draft them → share with you

- 3. You both draft → compare → agree on the best 6
	- (you’re the final decision maker)








## **🔑 Also, Remember the Difference between goals and responibilities**

In 365:
**Responsibilities = your 6 (areas you own)**  
**Projects = your 3 (goals for this cycle)**


## **🧱 How they work together**

- Your **6 responsibilities** = what you are always accountable for
- Your **3 goals** = what you are improving right now

👉 So:
**Your 3 goals should come FROM your 6 responsibilities**

---

## **📍 Example**

### **Responsibilities (the 6)**

- Guest follow-up
- Next Steps environment
- Volunteer team
- Data tracking
- Connection pathways
- Guest experience

### **Goals (the 3 projects for this cycle)**

- Build a new follow-up system
- Increase next steps conversion from 12% → 20%
- Recruit and train 10 new volunteers

Notice:
- Each goal ties back to a responsibility
- Nothing random
- Nothing disconnected



## **🧠 Why this matters**

Without this:

- Goals become random
- Roles feel scattered
- Teams lose focus

With this:

- Every project has a purpose
- Every role gets stronger each cycle


**“Your 6 define what you own.** **Your 3 define what you’re improving.”**
