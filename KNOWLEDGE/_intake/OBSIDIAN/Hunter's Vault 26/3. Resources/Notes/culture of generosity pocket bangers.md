# GENEROSITY IS A DISCIPLESHIP ISSUE

  
  

# Hook

Most pastors I talk to feel guilty every time they bring up generosity. Not because they're greedy. Because they're afraid it sounds like they just need money.  
  

# Build

Here's what changed everything for me:  
 Generosity isn't just a financial conversation.... It's a discipleship conversation.  
 Generosity isn't just about funding the church—it’s about forming disciples.  
  

  
When someone learns to give — really give — something happens in them.  
Their trust in God deepens. their need for control loosens. and their faith gets practical.

  
That's not a budget issue.  
That's a formation issue.

  
But most churches only talk about generosity when they need something.  
 A campaign. A shortfall. A building.

  
And people feel it. They know the difference between being developed and being leveraged.  
 So they give out of obligation. Or they don't give at all.  
 And we wonder why generosity feels like pulling teeth.  
  

# Payoff

The churches I've seen lead this well don't treat generosity like a financial ask. They treat it like a growth pathway.  
  

Because here's the truth: A person who isn't growing in generosity isn't fully growing as a disciple.

You can't pastor someone toward spiritual maturity and skip this topic.
