- A mission statement explains in a clear and effective way why the organisation exists (and why it should exist) and what it is supposed to do.
- Some Aspects Of A Mission Statement
  A mission statement is the why of the organisation.
  A mission statement should be short, clear and crisp.
  A mission statement guides the organisation when developing or reviewing the strategy.
  A mission statement is a key instrument when checking if the organisation is still doing what it supposed to do.
  A mission statement is a point of reference when the organisation faces major decisions.
- A mission statement can be used as a tool when exploring the market for mergers, acquisitions or competitors.
  A mission statement should be sufficiently broad allowing for growth.
  A mission statement primarily serves internal purposes. You could share it with a broader audience, but it will only distract you if you think about the broader audience when creating the mission statement.
  A mission statement is not inward looking.
  A mission statement will also be used for formal purposes (a proposal in a tender procedure, an application to government, tax purposes) but that should not impact you when creating the statement.
  A mission statement can be used for both profit and not-for-profit organisations, for small and large organisations, for new and existing organisations.
  A mission statement should be clear to all people in the organisation.

  Requirements And Definitions​ I feel this is the most difficult phase. You now need to describe the goal in terms that others understand and can relate to their own world. You think ‘fast and on line’. The IT manager will ask: how fast? What kind of down time is acceptable? And how about mean time between repair? Don’t rush your answer, this is where business and IT connect.​ A more exciting example: you want surveys to be flexible, allowing your customers to do all kinds of analysis on their data. You’re happy with flexible, fit for purpose but what does it actually mean? Suppose the IT person and you switch positions: what would you want to know?​ Definitions are like anchors. ​ ​ Business must also connect to finance, operations, marketing and all other functions in your organisation. Talk to these teams and explore what the impact is or what they require from the new system and vice versa.​ I guess you know start thinking: who is going to do all of the work?
