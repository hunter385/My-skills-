> "Man is pushed by drives," Viktor Frankl observed. "But he is pulled by values."
>
> Ruled by or ruling? Which are you?
>
> Without the right values, success is brief. If we wish to do more than flash, if we wish to last, then it is time to understand how to battle this new form of ego and what values and principles are required in order to beat it.

**Drives vs. Values**
Frankl's distinction highlights two forces that shape our behavior:
- **Drives** push us from behind—instincts, impulses, immediate needs, and ego reactions that we respond to automatically
- **Values** pull us forward—chosen principles and meaningful goals that we consciously move toward

**The Question of Control**
The question "Ruled by or ruling?" asks whether you're in the driver's seat of your life. Are you being controlled by external pressures, ego, and reactive patterns? Or are you actively choosing your direction based on what truly matters?

**Values as the Foundation for Lasting Success**
The passage suggests that without grounding yourself in the right values, any success will be temporary. Many people achieve short-term wins driven by ego, ambition, or external validation—they "flash" brightly but burn out. To build something enduring, you need:
- Clear values that guide decision-making
- Principles that help you navigate challenges
- The ability to recognize and manage ego's influence

**The Battle Ahead**
The reference to a "new form of ego" suggests this isn't just about traditional arrogance—it might be about more subtle forms like status-seeking, performance anxiety, or identity attachment that come with modern success. Lasting achievement requires understanding and actively working against these tendencies.
