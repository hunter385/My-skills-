> "Is this really a problem I need to think my way out of? Or is it possible I just need to fix my biochemistry?"

recognize the difference between psychological problems and physiological ones. Sometimes what feels like a mental or emotional issue that requires deep thinking or analysis is actually caused by physical factors—your biochemistry.

For example, issues like anxiety, poor focus, irritability, or feeling overwhelmed might not need complex problem-solving. Instead, they could be addressed by:
- Getting better sleep
- Eating properly
- Exercising
- Addressing nutritional deficiencies
- Managing stress through physical practices

The insight is to pause and ask: "Am I overthinking this, or do I just need to take care of my body?" It's a reminder that physical well-being directly affects mental state, and sometimes the simplest solution is the right one.
