# DESIGN YOUR SYSTEM

### START WHERE YOU ARE
**Review of action items for Step 1**
Here are the key action items to make sure you complete before finishing Step 1 (Start Where You Are):
- Do a full inventory of where your information currently lives. Make sure you include all apps, email accounts, and cloud files at work, school, and home.
- Figure out what works in your current setup.
- Get clear on your friction points and what isn't working in your current setup.

### DEFINE YOUR DESTINATION
**Review of action items for Step 2**
- Get clear on the difference that you hope that having a sustainable personal knowledge management system will make in your life.
- Brainstorm a full list of all the projects, commitments, and goals that are on your plate right now. Give some thought to the values underpinning each of these.
- Identify the types of information associated with each of these projects. Each project will have a different type and format of supporting material.
- Imagine the perfect solution to manage all your current notes, information, and documents. Dream big. Now try to imagine the 80% solution.

### DRAFT YOUR BLUEPRINT
**Review of action items for Step 3**
- Make your inventory of information you're trying to manage. Identify your formatted, unformatted (essential notes), and mixed information.
- Choose your approach to app structure: all-in-one or targeted.
- Find the right tool for the task.
- Reflect on which types of information (formatted, unformatted, or mixed) are necessary for each of your projects. Think about how you could best organize information for each project.

### SELECT YOUR APP
**Review of action items for Step 4**
- Keep the 5 key attributes of a sustainable PKM system in mind as you choose your apps.
- Think about which PKM persona(s) fit(s) you best.
- Make an outline or physical sketch of your planned PKM workflow.
- Browse the filtered lists in the PKM Master List to find the apps that best suit your own PKM needs, based on your identified goals and values.

### DESIGN YOUR LIBRARY
**Review of action items for Step 5**
- Reflect on how your mind naturally organizes information. Choose your structuring system: folders, tags, or bidirectional links.
- Set up a draft structure of this layout in your new app(s) that you chose in Step 4. Brainstorm a structure that will help your future self find what they're looking for.
- Set aside protected time to work through the 7-step process to import your existing notes and documents into your new system.
