### **1. The Deep Life Framework**
A structured approach to living intentionally and meaningfully, built around **four levels**:

1. **Discipline** – Mastering time, habits, and focus (Deep Work, time blocking, shutdown rituals).
2. **Values** – Clarifying what actually matters in your life.
3. **Control** – Regaining agency over your schedule, technology, and commitments.
4. **Vision** – Crafting a coherent, purpose-driven direction for your life.

Each level builds on the previous one: you can't create vision if your time and habits are chaotic.

---
### **2. The Deep Life Stack**
A practical method to implement the Deep Life across specific **domains**:
- **Craft** – Your work and professional mastery.
- **Community** – Relationships, family, friendships.
- **Constitution** – Health, fitness, and energy.
- **Contemplation** – Reflection, faith, and learning.
- **Celebration** – Joy, play, and gratitude.

Each domain has its own "experiments" — small lifestyle design tweaks tested for 30–60 days.

---
### **3. Time-Block Planning System**
- Split days into blocks of focused, intentional activity.
- Schedule **Deep Work** sessions first.
- Use "overflow" blocks for admin and shallow work.
- Review and adjust throughout the day ("adaptive blocks").

This system operationalizes the "Discipline" tier of the Deep Life.

---
### **4. The Deep Work Framework**
The foundational mindset for high-quality output:

- **Work deeply.** Train focus like a muscle.
- **Embrace boredom.** Resist switching to distraction.
- **Quit social media.** Reclaim attention from digital noise.
- **Drain the shallows.** Ruthlessly minimize low-value work.

---
### **5. The Career Capital Framework**
From *So Good They Can't Ignore You*:
- Build **rare and valuable skills** (career capital).
- Invest that capital into autonomy, mission, and impact.
- Avoid "follow your passion" — focus instead on **craftsmanship** and contribution.

---
### **6. The Digital Minimalism Philosophy**
- **Clarity:** Define what technology serves your deeply held values.
- **Control:** Remove or radically limit the rest.
- **Intentionality:** Reintroduce tools selectively and deliberately.
The 30-day digital declutter is a cornerstone practice here.

---
### **7. Slow Productivity Framework**
From his 2024 book:
- **Do fewer things.** Reduce the number of active projects.
- **Work at a natural pace.** Align energy with meaningful progress.
- **Obsess over quality.** Excellence compounds over time.
A rejection of "hustle culture" in favor of sustainable depth.

---
### **8. The Attention Capital Theory**
From *A World Without Email*:
- Attention is the **most valuable resource** in knowledge work.
- Replace ad-hoc messaging with **structured workflows** (process-centric collaboration).
- Build systems that protect focus across teams.

---
### **9. Lifestyle Design through Deep Habits**
- Introduce **one habit at a time**, rooted in identity and purpose.
- Track through reflection (weekly review, quarterly reset).
- Combine habits into "deep routines" — e.g., morning writing ritual, evening shutdown.

---
### **10. The Seasonal Reset**
- Every quarter, review what's working and what's not.
- Set one "deep project" focus per season.
- Revisit core commitments in each Deep Life domain.
