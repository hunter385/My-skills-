# WEEKLY TEMPLATE
---
The Ideal Week.
The Ideal Week serves as a bridge between your quarterly big picture plan and making weekly progress toward it.
These guidelines should be established during your quarterly planning to ensure consistent progress toward your goals.
Will your weekly structure realistically support accomplishing your plan?

# **TIME PROTECTION**
---
Time protection involves intentionally setting aside specific periods of time each week for predetermined activities.

> This time is always dedicated to this activity.

**EXAMPLES:**
- My mornings are for writing.
- My lunches are for exercise.
- My Tuesday afternoons from 3-5pm are for office hours. Instead of endless email exchanges or scheduling separate 1-on-1 meetings, I'm available during this time for quick, efficient requests from my team.

# **DAILY THEMES**
---
Daily themes are a strategy where different days of the week are dedicated to specific types of activities.

**EXAMPLES:**
- Mondays are for meeting days.
- Wednesdays are for deep work and focus time.
- Thursdays are for client projects.

# **QUOTAS**
---
Quotas are guidelines I'll enforce for the upcoming month to prevent overindulgence in areas that might hinder my progress towards my Living Plan.
- **Meeting Quota:** Set a maximum number of meetings you'll attend per week to ensure enough time for focused work.
- **Deep Work Hours Quota:** Set a minimum number of hours dedicated to deep, focused work each week.
- **Email Check Quota:** Limit the number of times you check your email each day to reduce distractions.

# OFFICE HOURS
---
Designated periods when I make myself available to anyone who needs to connect with me.
These time blocks are valuable because I can direct people to meet during these specific windows rather than scheduling separate meetings.
