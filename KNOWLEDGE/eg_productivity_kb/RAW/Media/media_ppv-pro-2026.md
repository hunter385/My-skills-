> PPV stands for Pillars, Pipelines, and Vaults. It is a connected Life Operating System that drives Focus, Alignment, and Knowledge resurfacing. The new Pro rebuild leverages Notion's modern databases for faster use, clearer structure, and smarter context surfacing.

# PPV's Primary Objectives
*Pillars, Pipelines, Vaults*
---
1. **Focus**: Sit down and do the work that matters now.
2. **Alignment**: Daily and weekly actions map to goals which map to life aspirations which map to pillars.
3. **Knowledge resurfacing**: Relevant resources return in the right place at the right time, while everything else stays out of the way.

# System Structure (top to bottom)
---
- **Pillars** → distilled values that you can memorize and revisit frequently.
- **Life Aspirations** → emotional, heart level outcomes that you want in life. Changeable as life evolves.
- **Goals** → 3, 6, 12 month objectives that make aspirations real. Time bound and specific.
- **Projects** → shorter initiatives that advance goals.
- **Routines** → designed recurring behaviors that also advance goals. Treated as first class citizens, not second to projects.
- **Actions** → concrete steps of 30 to 180 minutes. Actions roll up to projects or routines.
- **Vaults** → knowledge that fuels action and learning.

# The 3 Zones
---
### 1) Alignment Zone
- Home of **Pillars and Purpose**, **Life Aspirations**, **GPR** database for Goals, Projects, Routines, and the **Cycles** cadence.
- Autodata blocks use formulas and rollups to surface relevant counts and links inside each goal page.
- Visuals page shows progress with Notion charts and graphs.
- Identity and Mindset sculpting provide cognitive frameworks that serve progress.
### 2) Focus Zone
- Today view for doing the work.
- Plan the night before. Order by: urgent scheduled, quick items, deep focus 1 to 4, reminders.
- **Do date** is central. Every active action gets a do date. If you keep bumping an item, either do it or delete it.
- Compact daily list reduces overwhelm. Month view helps plan and reschedule realistically.
- Daily Entry page includes morning startup, end of day review, journaling, and tracking widgets.
- Specialty pipelines such as Content Zone and Workout tracking can live here.
### 3) Knowledge Zone
- **Topic Vault** replaces the older Knowledge Vault. Shows cross system context by topic.
- **Neurobits** is a single database for all captured items. Three segments: media, notes, documents. Each item links to topics and resurfaces in context.
- People database collects interactions, related actions, and resurfacing data.
- **Remembrance** uses a Notable toggle to save highlights by year for later reflection.

# Cycles Cadence
---
- **Annual** review and planning.
- **Duo Cycles** every two months to replace traditional quarters and months.
- **Weekly** reviews consolidate the past week and plan the next.
- **Daily** entries for planning and reflection. Data rolls up to weekly and duo cycles.

Purpose of cycles: prevent entropy, pull loose threads back into structure, and set the right next objectives.

# Quick Glossary
---
- **PPV**: Pillars, Pipelines, Vaults.
- **GPR**: Goals, Projects, Routines database.
- **Do date**: The day you intend to do the action.
- **Neurobits**: Single capture database for media, notes, and documents.
- **Topic Vault**: Cross system hub organized by topic.
- **Remembrance**: Yearly collection of Notable items to reflect on.
- **Duo Cycle**: Two month planning and review block.
