### **Big Idea**
- Most people manage work **reactively**:
	- Open inbox
	- Check Slack
	- Respond to whatever comes in
- Problem with reactive work:
	- You never feel "caught up"
	- **Urgent tasks crowd out important work**
- Time block planning flips the script:
	- You **decide in advance** how to spend your time
	- That intentionality changes productivity and stress levels

---
# **The Planner Layout (Daily Pages)**
Each day has two facing pages.

# **1) Right Page: Time Block Grid**
- This is where you create the **daily time block plan**
- Emphasis on using **paper**
	- It keeps planning **away from the computer**
	- Planning should happen **in isolation** from other work and distractions

**Morning Routine**
- First thing each morning:
	- Write the **date**
	- Mark working hours on the left side of the grid (example: 9am to 3pm)

**Building the Time Block Plan**
- Assign specific work to specific time blocks
- Example workflow:
	- **9:00–10:30** = Work on an important report (best concentration early)
	- **10:30–11:00** = Email
	- **11:00–12:30** = Meeting (real plan would include who it's with)
	- **12:30–1:30** = Return to report
	- **1:30–2:00** = Late lunch
	- **2:00–2:30** = Short tasks
	- **2:30–3:00** = More email

**Handling Small Tasks Without Space**
- If a block is too small to list tasks in the grid:
	- Label the block with a **number**
	- Use the **upper right space** to write the details for that numbered block

## **Why Time Blocking Works (Key Advantages)**
### **1) Better use of available time**
- You see the whole day as a resource
- You allocate time intentionally
- Benefits:
	- More gets done
	- Higher quality work
	- You can:
		- Fill small gaps with useful tasks (instead of web surfing)
		- Align tasks with your energy patterns (deep work when sharp, shallow work later)
### **2) Stronger defense against distraction**
- You always know what you are supposed to be doing
- Distraction becomes costly:
	- Web surfing = falling behind
	- Social media rabbit holes = schedule breaks
- The plan creates **accountability in the moment**
### **3) Realistic understanding of time**
- You learn how long work *actually* takes
- Example:
	- You estimate reports take 90 minutes
	- But repeatedly you go over
	- You're forced to confront reality and adjust (reports take 2 hours)
- You also see clearly how little time is left if you overcommit to meetings
- Newport claims:
	- Time block planners get **about 2× more done per day** than colleagues who don't

# **What to Do When You Bust the Schedule**
Busting the schedule is normal.

### **The Correct Method: Fix the Plan**
- If something runs over (meeting goes past its block):
	1. Move to the **next column**
	2. Redraw the block to reflect what actually happened
	3. Create a new plan for the remaining time
	4. Cross out the original plan that's no longer valid

**Key principle**
- You don't win for accuracy
- You win for intention
- The goal is not to follow the schedule perfectly
- The goal is to **always have a plan for the time that remains**

# **2) Left Page: Collection + Metrics + Shutdown**
### **A) Collection Space**
- Used for capturing ideas or tasks *without* breaking focus
- If something pops into your head during a work block:
	- Write it down in the collection area
	- Stay in the block
- You avoid:
	- Opening inbox
	- Launching apps
	- Getting pulled into "reactive mode"
### **B) Daily Metrics**
- At the end of the day, record key behaviors tied to success
- Examples:
	- Deep Work hours (DW)
		- Tally how many hours of deep work you actually did
	- Sales example:
		- Cold calls (CC)
		- Record number completed
- Why metrics matter:
	- Creates a record of progress
	- Adds psychological motivation:
		- Knowing you must write it down makes you more likely to do it
		- No one wants to write a big "0" where it can't be ignored
### **C) Shutdown Complete Checkbox**
- End your day with a clear finish signal:
	- Put an **X** in the "shutdown complete" checkbox
- Why it works:
	- Later anxiety tries to reopen work:
		- "Do we have a plan? Are we missing something?"
	- You can respond:
		- "Shutdown complete was checked."
		- That means a real shutdown ritual happened
		- No need to ruminate
- Effect:
	- Significantly reduces work-related anxiety
	- Clear boundary between work and rest
