# **Spiritual Domain**
**"Rooted and Growing"**
---
1. What does a deeply fulfilling spiritual life look like for you in the next 3–5 years?
2. How do you want to feel when you wake up each morning in terms of peace, purpose, or presence?
3. What spiritual habits or rhythms anchor you when life gets busy?
4. Where do you feel closest to God — in solitude, in Scripture, in service, or in community?
5. What kind of person do you want to become because of your walk with Christ?
6. When your faith feels dry, what helps rekindle it?
7. What mentors or spiritual voices guide you most clearly?
8. How do you want to invest in others spiritually — mentoring, teaching, serving?
9. What would "fruitfulness" look like in this season?
10. Are your daily habits aligned with the kind of peace you want to experience?
11. What might you need to prune from your life to grow deeper roots?
12. How do you want worship to feel — formal, free, habitual, heartfelt?
13. What scriptures, prayers, or truths do you want to live from daily?
14. What environment or rhythm most helps you hear God's voice clearly?
15. When people describe your faith, what do you hope they say?

<details>
<summary>2025-10-08 draft</summary>

**Vision**
> I live in an unhurried, ongoing relationship with the Lord. I hear His voice clearly and stay in sync with His Spirit. My days feel conversational — I sense His nearness and respond to His nudges. There's no distance, no barrier. I know His heart and speak life over others from that place of intimacy.

**Healthy Rhythms**
- Create slow, unhurried space with God daily.
- Process thoughts and prayers *in writing* — don't just think them.
- Keep short accounts — confess and realign quickly when sin or distance creeps in.
- Protect the relationship like you would a closest friend: consistent time, deep honesty, shared joy.

**Red Flags (when I drift)**
- I'm rushing through prayer or skipping journaling.
- My heart feels foggy or disconnected.
- I'm living reactive instead of responsive to His Spirit.
</details>

# **Personal Domain**
**"Healthy, Whole, and Focused"**
---
1. What kind of person do you want to be — physically, mentally, emotionally?
2. What does a "healthy lifestyle" mean to you beyond diet and exercise?
3. How do you recharge when you're depleted?
4. What hobbies, adventures, or creative outlets light you up?
5. What does your ideal morning routine look like?
6. What boundaries help you stay grounded?
7. Where are you currently overextended or undernourished?
8. How do you want to handle stress, conflict, or fatigue differently?
9. What habits would most change your energy or mindset if they became consistent?
10. How do you want to grow intellectually — through books, courses, mentors, or travel?
11. If you had a completely free day each week, how would you spend it?
12. How do you want to steward your finances in a way that reflects your values?
13. What personal wins would make you proud one year from now?
14. What area of your character do you most want to strengthen?
15. What does "rest" actually look like for you — and how often do you practice it?

<details>
<summary>2025-10-08 draft</summary>

> I feel strong, confident, and clear. I love the body I see in the mirror because it reflects discipline and health. My mind stays sharp through steady learning — a book a week keeps me growing. Emotionally, I'm grounded and self-aware. I handle stress and temptation in healthy ways. I know what to do when I feel off, and I return quickly to center.

**Healthy Rhythms**
- Run every day and get outside in the sun.
- Play pickleball often — movement + joy together.
- Read one book a week to stay sharp and inspired.
- Practice emotional awareness daily — name what I feel and choose healthy responses.
- Keep a "coping plan" nearby for moments of temptation, fatigue, or anxiety.
- Protect sleep and recovery as seriously as work.

**Red Flags (when I drift)**
- Using quick fixes (like porn, scrolling, or overeating) instead of addressing real needs.
- Feeling foggy or unmotivated to learn.
- Skipping exercise or losing outdoor time.
- Reacting instead of reflecting when emotions rise.
</details>

# **Relational Domain**
**"Deep, Life-Giving Connections"**
---
1. Who are the people you most want to be intentional with this year?
2. What do your closest relationships need from you right now?
3. How do you want people to feel after spending time with you?
4. What kind of friend/spouse/parent/leader do you want to become?
5. Where have you been reactive instead of relational lately?
6. What habits or rhythms could strengthen your family life?
7. Who do you need to forgive, reconcile with, or thank?
8. What kind of community do you want around you — mentors, peers, apprentices?
9. What conversations have you been avoiding that would actually bring health?
10. How do you want to celebrate and show gratitude more consistently?
11. How could you be more fully present with the people who matter most?
12. What relational boundaries need to be clarified or reset?
13. How do you want to love others sacrificially without losing yourself?
14. What legacy do you want your relationships to leave behind?
15. When people talk about the atmosphere of your home or team, what do you want them to describe?

**Vision**
> My relationships are rich, intentional, and full of life. I cultivate closeness with Emily, Charlie, and Teddy through shared time, laughter, and purpose. I'm a creative initiator — designing experiences, not just reacting to them. My friends feel uplifted, believed in, and inspired after being with me. My humor builds, not cuts. I'm an encourager to my wife, a mentor to my kids, and a friend who calls greatness out of others.

**Healthy Rhythms**
- Initiate family experiences monthly — trips, traditions, or simple adventures.
- Set aside one-on-one time weekly with Emily and each child.
- Cultivate friendship with Tylor, Hannah, and Andrew through shared experiences and intentional contact.
- Use words to *build up* — replace cutting humor with life-giving language.
- Keep a short list of "people I'm pouring into" and check in regularly.
- Practice presence — put the phone away and engage deeply.

**Red Flags (when I drift)**
- I'm reacting to others instead of creating moments with them.
- Humor turns sarcastic or critical.
- Family time feels rushed or transactional.
- I'm isolated or missing connection with my closest friends.
</details>

# **Vocational Domain**
**"Purposeful Work that Multiplies Impact"**
---
1. What kind of impact do you want your work or ministry to create over the next 5 years?
2. How do you want your daily work to feel — energizing, strategic, creative, relational?
3. What would a truly "deep work" day look like for you?
4. Where do you want to be known for excellence or mastery?
5. What parts of your work give you life, and which parts drain it?
6. Who do you want to mentor, multiply, or raise up in your field?
7. What problem are you uniquely wired to solve?
8. If you could design your role from scratch, what would stay and what would go?
9. What kind of leadership culture do you want to build around you?
10. How do you want to measure "success" — by outcomes, impact, joy, or faithfulness?
11. What skill, tool, or mindset do you need to level up next?
12. How do you want to integrate your calling with the rest of your life — not compete with it?
13. What are your "non-negotiables" in the way you lead and serve?
14. How do you want to finish each week feeling — drained or deeply satisfied?
15. If God gave you one bold assignment in this next season, what might it be?

<details>
<summary>2025-10-08 draft</summary>

**Vision**
> My work multiplies kingdom impact and transforms how churches grow. I help 100 churches break 1,000 in attendance — reaching 100,000 lives weekly. My days feel creative, strategic, and deeply satisfying. I lead from inspiration, not exhaustion. I'm known for my writing, teaching, and thought leadership that equips organizations to thrive.

**Healthy Rhythms**
- Block daily "Deep Work" time — protect it as sacred.
- Design and maintain an inspiring workspace: natural light, music, pour-over coffee, aesthetic simplicity.
- Write and publish regularly — ideas, lessons, frameworks.
- Lead high-value workshops for private clients in a beautiful, intentional setting.
- Track church impact and celebrate milestones (e.g., each church that breaks 1,000).
- Keep margin for creative thinking, reading, and idea incubation.

**Red Flags (when I drift)**
- Working reactively instead of strategically.
- Creative output feels forced or thin.
- Workspace is cluttered or uninspiring.
- Losing sight of measurable impact (stories and numbers).
</details>
