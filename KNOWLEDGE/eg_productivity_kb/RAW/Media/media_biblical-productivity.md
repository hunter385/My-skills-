# **The Self Help Lie**
## **Big Idea**
Secular self help assumes: **"You belong to yourself."**
Biblical truth: **You belong to God.**
Christian productivity must be built on a Scriptural foundation before habits, goals, or tactics will matter.

---
# **Pillar 1: You Belong to God**
**Origin of Christian Productivity**
- Secular mindset: "It is my life. Productivity is optional and mainly for my own happiness."
- Biblical mindset: **You are not your own. You were bought with a price** (1 Corinthians 6).
- John 15: We are branches connected to Christ the true vine.
- If you belong to God, then growth is not optional.
- Stewardship replaces self ownership.
- The question is not "How do I get more out of life for myself?"
	It becomes: **"How do I bring a good return to Christ, my Master?"**

---
# **Pillar 2: You Are Empowered by God**
**Source of Christian Productivity**
- John 15: Apart from Christ you can do nothing.
- Believers have union with Christ. Christ is in us through the Holy Spirit.
- Fruitfulness comes from connection to the vine, not from self willpower.
- Philippians 2:13: **God works in you to will and to work for his good pleasure.**
- Secular productivity: Power comes from within yourself.
- Christian productivity: Power comes from God working in you.
- Since the power belongs to God, **He gets the glory** for any fruit you bear.

---
# **Pillar 3: You Exist to Glorify God**
**Purpose of Christian Productivity**
- John 15:8: **By this my Father is glorified, that you bear much fruit.**
- Glorifying God is making much of Him through:
	- Worship
	- Attitudes that reflect His character
	- Actions that display His greatness
- Thomas Watson: Glory is the sparkling of divinity.
- Productivity becomes a way to magnify God, not yourself.
- 1 Corinthians 10:31: Do all things for the glory of God.
- Secular purpose: Be productive to become successful, happy, or comfortable.
- Biblical purpose: **Be productive to glorify God.**

---
# **Pillar 4: You Were Saved to Work for God**
**Content of Christian Productivity**
- John 15:16: Christ appointed you to bear fruit.
- Titus 2:14: Christ redeemed a people who are **zealous for good works.**
- Salvation is by grace alone, but good works are the necessary consequence of salvation (Ephesians 2:10).
- Believers become new creations able to serve God and reflect His image.
- Your productivity is the pursuit of **good works** done in the strength of Christ.
- This includes everyday disciplines:
	- Keeping your word
	- Using time wisely
	- Prayer, Scripture, caring for your body
	- Fulfilling responsibilities with a God honoring attitude
- Secular focus: Be productive to chase your dreams.
- Biblical focus: **Be productive to follow Christ and bear good fruit.**

---
# **Pillar 5: You Will Stand Before God**
**Motivation of Christian Productivity**
- John 15:16 speaks of fruit that will abide.
- 1 Corinthians 3: Good works built on the foundation of Christ will survive the fire of judgment.
- Romans 14:12: Each believer will give an account to God.
- This is not judgment for salvation. It is judgment for reward.
- Matthew 6:19-20: Lay up treasure in heaven, not on earth.
- Secular mindset: Be productive to get more out of this life.
- Christian mindset: **Be productive to gain more in the next life.**
- Eternal reward reframes daily discipline.
- Productivity becomes inspiring rather than burdensome.

---
# **Summary of the Five Pillars**
1. **You belong to God**
2. **You are empowered by God**
3. **You exist to glorify God**
4. **You were saved to work for God**
5. **You will stand before God**

These form the foundation for all tactics like goal setting, habit building, planning systems, and task management. Without this foundation, productivity will drift back to self focus.
