# **Five Keys to Faithful Time Stewardship**
Productivity from a Christian worldview

## **Overview**
Productivity often feels overwhelming because it involves goals, habits, projects, time management and various systems. This teaching offers five simple, biblical strategies you can apply *this week* to steward your time in a way that glorifies God.

---
# **1. Start With Why**

### **The Ultimate Why: The Glory of God**
- Our productivity exists to *make God look good*
	- *1 Corinthians 10:31* — "Whatever you do, do all to the glory of God."
- Secular productivity focuses on goals, projects and tasks.
	Christian productivity starts with the biggest why: **God's glory**.

### **The Productivity Pyramid**
**Biblical Model**
1. **God's Glory**
2. **Domains of Stewardship**
	- Spiritual
	- Relational
	- Vocational
	- Physical
	- Financial
	- Recreational
	- Intellectual
3. **Goals**
4. **Projects**
5. **Tasks and Habits**

**Why this matters:**
- You glorify God not in the abstract, but by being faithful in the specific domains he has entrusted to you.
- Evaluate each domain regularly (quarterly is suggested).
	- Rank current faithfulness 1 to 5.
	- Identify gaps.
	- Set goals to grow in faithfulness.

### **Key Idea**
Faithful time stewardship aligns tasks ← projects ← goals ← stewardship ← God's glory.
The "silver thread" through all actions is the glory of God.

---
# **2. Set Clear Goals**

### **Purpose of Goals**
Goals define *what truly matters right now*:
- They give direction.
- They reveal priorities.
- They give you a reason to say "no."
- They are what you manage time *for*.
Without clear goals, everything feels urgent, nothing feels important and time management collapses.

### **Write It Down**
- Study in the *British Journal of Health Psychology*:
	**Writing down when and where** you will do something dramatically increases follow-through.
	- Control group: 38 percent follow-through
	- Motivational material group: 35 percent
	- "Write down when and where" group: **91 percent**

### **Practical Application**
- Define goals with precision.
	- Not: "I want to lose weight."
	- Instead: "Walk 30 minutes at 7 am Monday through Friday."
- Ask: **How does this goal glorify God?**
	Usually through faithful stewardship of a domain.

---
# **3. Make Specific Plans**

### **Why Plans Matter**
- *Proverbs 16:3* — "Commit your work to the Lord, and your plans will be established."
- In Proverbs, planning is a mark of wisdom.
	Failing to plan marks foolishness.

### **Projects = Your Plan of Attack**
A project is a structured plan that moves you toward a goal.
Benefits:
- **Creates urgency** through deadlines
- **Clarifies steps** so you aren't rethinking everything each day
- **Builds momentum** through visible progress
- **Allows "action-mode you" to simply execute** rather than plan on the fly

### **Practical Steps**
- Take one hour to plan.
- Break the goal into projects.
- Break projects into tasks and habits.
- Identify obstacles and needed resources.
Good planning makes execution dramatically easier.

---
# **4. Schedule Your Week**

### **Why Weekly Scheduling Matters**
Weekly planning is where you begin to *actually steward time* around your goals and God-given responsibilities.
Most people try to manage their time without:
- A why
- A goal
- A plan
- A structure
And they wonder why it feels chaotic.

### **How to Build a Weekly Schedule**

### **A. Theme Your Days**
Assign broad purposes to different days.
Examples:
- Wednesday → record videos
- Monday/Friday → admin work
- Homemaker example:
	- Tuesday → meal prep
	- Wednesday → deep cleaning

### **B. Map Your Energy Levels**
From Carey Nieuwhof's *At Your Best*:
- **Green Zone**: High energy, high focus
- **Yellow Zone**: Moderate energy
- **Red Zone**: Low energy, low creativity
Match tasks accordingly:
- Green → deep work, writing, thinking
- Yellow → meetings, moderate-focus activities
- Red → admin, email, repetitive tasks

### **C. Fill the Schedule by Priority**
- Add non-negotiables first: sleep, meals, work blocks
- Insert high-priority work into green zones
- Add exercise, relationships, family time, housework, recreation
- Then fill remaining time with lower-priority tasks

### **D. Categorize by Domains of Stewardship**
Color-coding helps you see imbalances:
- Are you giving time to relationships?
- To your body?
- To spiritual disciplines?
A weekly schedule gives structure, clarity and balance to life.

---
# **5. Prioritize Necessary Rest**

### **Principles of Rest**

### **A. Beware of Burnout**
Pushing at full capacity without rest leads to collapse and undermines long-term faithfulness.

### **B. Reject the Myth of Perfect Efficiency**
- You are not a machine.
- You are more like a plant growing fruit than a robot making widgets.
- Energy fluctuates.
- Rest is part of productivity, not the enemy of it.

### **C. Steward All Domains**
- Faithfulness is holistic.
- You cannot glorify God while neglecting your body, relationships or emotional health.

### **D. Embrace Creatureliness**
- *Psalm 103:13–14* — God shows compassion because he knows we are dust.
- God does not expect us to be limitless.
- We should not place expectations on ourselves that God himself does not put on us.
- Rest is worship.
- Enjoying the fruit of your labor glorifies him.

---
# **Summary: The Five Keys**
1. **Start with why**
	- The glory of God is the foundation.
2. **Set clear goals**
	- Define what matters most right now.
3. **Make specific plans**
	- Create actionable steps to reach your goals.
4. **Schedule your week**
	- Structure your time around what matters most.
5. **Prioritize necessary rest**
	- Rest is part of faithful stewardship.
Apply even one of these this week and watch what the Lord does with your faithfulness.
