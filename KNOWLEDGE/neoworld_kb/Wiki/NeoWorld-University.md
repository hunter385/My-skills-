## The reframe

Most people watching Dillon stream on Whatnot wonder if they could do it too. Most who try, can't sustain it. The gap is not the platform — it's the playbook. NeoWorld University sells the playbook.

## The product

- **Price:** $50/month subscription.
- **Target customer:** Aspiring Whatnot streamers. Any niche — not Pokemon-specific. NeoWorld is the proof model, but the playbook is platform-general.
- **Delivery:** Weekly live coaching calls (Q&A format) + a private community.
- **North-star outcome:** Become a full-time streamer. Following NeoWorld's example as the working proof that the model can support a real income.

## What the customer is actually paying for

The $50/mo customer is paying for two things:

1. **Direct access to Dillon and the team during weekly calls.** Q&A means they bring the real problems they're hitting (low viewer count, bad inventory, slow auction, shipping bottleneck, etc.) and get real-time answers from people running the model at $4.5M.
2. **A community of people on the same path.** The private community is the "find your people" layer — peer accountability, deal-sharing, learnings.

What it's NOT (yet):

- Async course library (curriculum exists but isn't the headline product).
- One-on-one coaching.
- Done-for-you services.

## Launch path (as of 2026-05-30)

**Launch strategy: free → paid funnel, not a hard launch date.**

- **Four free pre-NeoWorld-University coaching calls** running on Whatnot.
- **400+ people have bookmarked the shows** — strong pre-launch interest signal.
- **Sales mechanic:** convert free-call attendees into $50/mo paid subscribers.

This is smart for three reasons:

1. **Distribution piggybacks on the existing audience.** No separate ad spend to drive launch traffic — the existing Whatnot audience becomes the funnel.
2. **Live calls demonstrate the product.** Aspiring streamers experience the format and value before paying.
3. **No artificial scarcity.** Subscribers can join when ready, not when a launch window opens.

The 400+ bookmark count is a strong leading indicator. Even a 5% conversion = 20 paying subscribers = $1k/mo recurring. 10% = $2k/mo. The free calls are running a real-world conversion test.

## How University connects to the broader business

NeoWorld University is the **second revenue line** layered on top of the auction business. It's also a strategic hedge: revenue from Dillon's stream hours is capped by stream hours. Subscription revenue scales without adding stream hours.

If the University grows to 1,000 subscribers at $50/mo = $50k/month = $600k/year. Not a replacement for the core business — a meaningful margin contributor with very different economics.
