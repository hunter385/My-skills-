# Hunter — COO Role at NeoWorld

**Status:** established
**Last updated:** 2026-05-30
**Sources:** Hunter interview 2026-05-30, [[NeoWorld-Team]]

## The role in one line

Hunter is the operations + strategic architect of NeoWorld. Off-camera, back-office. Working through Cycle Rocks with Laura. Frequent loops with Dillon.

## The day-to-day shape

- **Weekly team meeting** with the whole team.
- **Cycle Rock execution lives in Basecamp** — the shared team project tool. (Aligns with Hunter's stated taste love for the 37signals worldview.)
- **Frequent calls + in-person time with Dillon** — both strategic alignment and operational unblock.
- **Not on stream.** Hunter doesn't appear on Whatnot. The on-camera presence is Dillon (and Ian for the second stream slot).

## What this role owns

From the team picture in [[NeoWorld-Team]]:

- **Operations leadership** — the systems and frameworks that keep the four pillars (Streaming, Buying, Shipping, Coaching Calls) operating.
- **Cycle Rock partnership with Laura** — projects assigned by best-fit, not by title boundary.
- **Software tools** — building the operational infrastructure (CRM-ish needs, ops dashboards, internal automations).
- **Frameworks** — applying the Middle Method (in monthly cadence — see [[Monthly-Cycle]]) and other RSG-derived systems to NeoWorld's growth.
- **Strategic input on the three growth levers** — Brand Deals, NeoWorld University, operational efficiency.

## What this role doesn't own

- **The buying decisions.** Dillon + Ian buy; Hunter + Laura are formalizing the budget and the framework, not deciding card by card.
- **The streaming output.** Dillon and Ian are the on-camera operators.
- **The financial model.** Laura owns acting CFO duties; Hunter inputs but Laura owns.
- **Shipping operations.** Ian oversees; contractors execute.

## How NeoWorld fits Hunter's broader life

This is Hunter's second business — he's also CEO of Ready Set Grow ($4M consulting + mastermind for pastors). The two businesses run on different Cycle cadences (RSG = 6 weeks, NeoWorld = monthly) and serve different audiences (pastors vs. streamers), but they share Hunter as the operator and the Middle Method as the operating system.

Hunter's overall capacity allocation isn't formally documented yet. Worth a future capture — how does he split his calendar between RSG and NeoWorld inside a given week?

## The 37signals fingerprint

Two signals that Hunter's voice profile shows up in how NeoWorld runs:

1. **Basecamp as the team tool.** Hunter's stated taste love for the 37signals worldview is expressed in tool choice.
2. **Monthly cycles ("work in pencil, not ink").** The cadence isn't 90 days, partly because the market doesn't allow it and partly because Hunter believes in short cycles over long-horizon planning. Both businesses run faster cycles than what RSG teaches churches.

These aren't accidents — they're Hunter's philosophy made operational.
