# Monthly Cycle (NeoWorld's Middle Method)

**Status:** established
**Last updated:** 2026-05-30
**Sources:** Hunter interview 2026-05-30; [[rsg_kb/Wiki/Middle-Method]] (parent framework)

## The reframe

NeoWorld doesn't run on 90-day cycles. It can't. Streaming inventory, Whatnot platform dynamics, and Pokemon card market price movements all turn over too fast. A 90-day Cycle Rock would be three lifetimes of market shift.

So NeoWorld runs the Middle Method at **monthly cadence** instead.

The principle is unchanged — Cycles fight distraction, Cooldowns fight misalignment. The duration is just tuned to the speed of the business.

## The framework at monthly cadence

| Layer | RSG teaches (church) | RSG runs (own business) | NeoWorld runs |
|-------|----------------------|--------------------------|---------------|
| Cycle length | 90 days | 6 weeks | 1 month |
| Cooldown | 30 days | ~1 week | Embedded in month |
| Cycle Rocks | 3 | 3 | 3 |
| Weekly 3 / Daily 3 | Yes | Yes | Yes |

For NeoWorld specifically:

- **Month 1, Week 1:** New Cycle starts. Confirm 3 Cycle Rocks for the month. Hunter + Laura partner on what each owns.
- **Weeks 1–4:** Execute. Weekly 3 / Daily 3 against the Rocks. No new ideas dumped mid-month.
- **End of month:** Cooldown — review what shipped, what didn't, what carries. Reset Rocks for the next month.

## Why monthly works for NeoWorld

Three reasons the monthly cadence is right for this business:

1. **Inventory cycles fast.** A buy decision made in week 1 hits the stream by week 2 and the financial outcome is visible by week 3. Feedback loops are tight enough to learn inside a month.
2. **Market dynamics shift.** Pokemon card market prices, Whatnot platform changes, viewer trends — all move at speeds 90-day cycles can't track.
3. **Operational tempo is high.** 45 streaming hours/week + shipping volume + buying decisions = a lot of operational decisions per month. Compressing reflection into monthly increments keeps the team learning.

## Why NeoWorld can't run on RSG's 6-week cadence

RSG's 6-week cycles work for a content business where the publishing rhythm is weekly. NeoWorld's "publishing rhythm" is hourly (live streams). 6 weeks = ~270 stream-hours. Too long to wait before resetting priorities.

Monthly is the longest cadence NeoWorld's market allows.

## Cooldown protocol (monthly version)

Cooldown is shorter than RSG's — it can't take a week off the streaming schedule. NeoWorld's Cooldown is embedded in the last few days of the month + the first day of the next:

- **Last Thursday/Friday:** Cycle review. What got done. What didn't. What's the lesson.
- **First Monday of next month:** Next Cycle's 3 Rocks confirmed. Communicate to team. Lock in.

The streaming schedule never breaks. Cooldown happens around the edges.
