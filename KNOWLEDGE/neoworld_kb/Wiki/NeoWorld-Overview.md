# NeoWorld — Overview

**Status:** established
**Last updated:** 2026-05-30
**Sources:** Hunter interview 2026-05-30, [[NeoWorld Growth Plan.md]], [[Neoworld business model canvas.md]], [[NeoWorld Frameworks.md]], [[Join the NeoWorld Team.md]]

## What NeoWorld is

NeoWorld is a Pokemon card streaming + sales business on Whatnot. The model:

- Two streamers go live on Whatnot 45 hours/week combined (Dillon 35, Ian 10).
- Inventory is bought specifically to stream — buying is downstream of streaming strategy.
- 100% of revenue today comes from card auctions during streams.
- An education product line — NeoWorld University — is launching to teach aspiring Whatnot streamers the same model.

## The team

Coaching calls (the customer-facing one — see [[NeoWorld-University]]) are the delivery mechanism for the University product.

## The numbers

- **2026 projection:** $4.5M revenue.
- **2026 stretch goal:** $5.5M revenue, ≥$750k profit.

Three growth levers drive the stretch:

1. **Brand deals** — new revenue line.
2. **NeoWorld University** — $50/mo subscription product.
3. **Operational efficiency** with Hunter and Laura on the team.

## Cadence

NeoWorld runs on **monthly cycles** (the [[Monthly-Cycle]] adaptation of the Middle Method). The streaming + inventory market turns over too fast for 90-day cycles. Each month = one Cycle.

## The strategic shift in progress

The original thesis (`The Problem.md`) framed scale as multiplying Dillon — *"Build 5 people who are 80% as good as you."* That thesis has evolved. The current scale plan leans harder on:

- Brand deals (asymmetric upside vs. linear streamer-hour scaling).
- NeoWorld University (turning the streamer-knowledge into a product, not just multiplying streamers in-house).
- Operational efficiency (margin compounds without adding streamer hours).

See [[Streamer-Multiplication]] for the evolution.

## How NeoWorld relates to Ready Set Grow

NeoWorld and RSG are separate businesses. Different audiences (streamers vs. pastors), different products (cards + coaching vs. consulting + mastermind), different cycle cadences (monthly vs. 6-week).

What they share: Hunter, his frameworks (the Middle Method works inside both), and his operating philosophy.

Don't surface NeoWorld content in RSG public outputs without explicit instruction. The two businesses stay in their own lanes for content distribution.
