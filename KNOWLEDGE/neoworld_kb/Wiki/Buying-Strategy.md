## Where buying stands today

The buying model is in transition. With Laura (Operations + CFO) and Hunter (COO) on the team, a formal buying budget is being built for the first time. Pre-team era was less structured — Dillon and Ian bought based on instinct and stream demand without a hardened framework.

The Growth Plan flagged this explicitly: *"if we could figure out a way to know the number of cards bought and sold, we could develop a model for the average cost & sales price and try to improve profitability."* Card counting machine + receipt tracking is the implementation move.

## Strategic principles

1. **Buy-to-stream, not stream-what-we-have.** Inventory is sourced specifically for streams. Buying decisions are downstream of streaming strategy — what plays well live, what generates auction excitement, what the audience expects.

2. **Margin compounds at this layer.** Per [[Whatnot-Mechanics]], every percentage point of buying margin compounds against the $4.05M post-platform-take revenue base. A 5% improvement in buying costs is $200k+ to the bottom line.

3. **Quantity thresholds matter.** Targets are all "quantities above 20" — buying in volume is the discipline. One-offs at the right price aren't worth the operational overhead of sourcing.

4. **Price ranges, not fixed prices.** The buy menu uses ranges. Below the low end = great buy. Above the high end = pass. Inside the range = depends on the rest of the inventory mix.

## The Growth Plan's note on buying volume

From `NeoWorld Growth Plan.md`:

> "Don't buy $150k worth of inventory. Buy $50k reserves."

The discipline is **buying for current and next stream, not warehousing inventory.** Cash tied up in unsold sealed product is cash not earning. The buy budget Laura is building should enforce this.
