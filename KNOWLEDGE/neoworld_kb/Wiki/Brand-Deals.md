# Brand Deals

**Status:** emerging
**Last updated:** 2026-05-30
**Sources:** Hunter interview 2026-05-30

## Why this is a strategic priority

Brand deals are the **highest-margin revenue line in the model.**

- Auction revenue pays a 10% Whatnot tax (per [[Whatnot-Mechanics]]). Brand deal revenue keeps 100%.
- Auction revenue scales linearly with stream hours. Brand deal revenue scales with audience and brand fit — asymmetric upside.
- Auction revenue depends on inventory cost + buying skill. Brand deal revenue is a margin business with near-zero variable cost.

If the $5.5M stretch goal + $750k profit lands, brand deals will likely be one of the largest contributors per dollar of revenue.

## The target sponsor pool (three categories in play)

### a. Pokemon-specific brands

TCG companies and Pokemon-card-specific vendors. The most natural fit — these brands want exposure to a hot Pokemon-buying audience.

Potential targets:
- Grading services (PSA, BGS, CGC) — every collector wants graded cards.
- Card storage / display brands (BCW, Ultra PRO, etc.).
- Trading card distributors and resellers.

### b. General gaming / collectibles brands

Adjacent to Pokemon but broader. Gaming, hobby, collectibles companies that want exposure to a 45-hour-a-week-streaming, deeply-engaged collector audience.

Potential targets:
- Gaming peripheral brands.
- Other TCG ecosystems (Magic, YuGiOh) — overlap audience.
- Hobby/maker brands (e.g., Ugmonk-style accessories).

### c. Influencer-economy brands

Apparel, accessories, lifestyle. The audience is identifying with Dillon's persona, not just the cards.

Potential targets:
- Streetwear / lifestyle apparel.
- Tech accessories (mechanical keyboards, headphones, lighting — Hunter's own taste vector).
- Productivity / creator-tools (something Hunter has direct connections to via RSG-adjacent work).

## Deal shapes

Two structures are in play:

1. **Single-stream sponsorship.** One stream, one brand, one paycheck. Lower commitment, faster cycle, good for testing new sponsors.
2. **Ongoing deals.** Multi-month or annual sponsorship. Higher revenue per relationship, predictable cash flow, deeper brand integration into the stream format.

The likely playbook: test sponsors with single-stream deals first, convert the winners to ongoing relationships.

## Strategic implications

1. **Audience size + engagement is the asset.** Brand deals scale on the value of NeoWorld's audience, not on stream hours. Growing the audience is upstream of growing brand deal revenue.
2. **Inventory should reinforce, not compete.** A sponsor's brand should align with the stream's existing aesthetic — sketchy off-brand sponsorships dilute the audience trust that brand deals are paying for.
3. **Whatnot platform tools matter.** Whatnot likely offers featured-show placement, push notifications, and other distribution boosts. Larger brand deals should fund Whatnot's promotional tools in addition to NeoWorld's pocket. (Worth confirming — see Whatnot-Mechanics Open Questions.)
