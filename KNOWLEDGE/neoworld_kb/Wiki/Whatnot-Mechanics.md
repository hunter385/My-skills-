# Whatnot Mechanics

**Status:** established (basic)
**Last updated:** 2026-05-30
**Sources:** Hunter interview 2026-05-30

## The economics in one line

**Whatnot takes 10% of every sale.** That's the platform tax NeoWorld pays for distribution.

## What this means for the model

At a $4.5M projected 2026 revenue:

- **Whatnot take:** ~$450k.
- **Net to NeoWorld before COGS:** ~$4.05M.

Then subtract:
- Card inventory cost (the cards Dillon + Ian buy to stream).
- Shipping costs (contractors + materials).
- Team salaries / contractor pay.
- Platform tools, software, overhead.

That's the path to the $750k profit stretch goal — every percentage point of operational efficiency or buying margin compounds against the $4.05M base.

## Strategic implications

1. **Margin compounds at the buying layer.** If buying gets 5% cheaper, that's $200k+ on the bottom line at $4.5M. The buying budget Laura and Hunter are now formalizing is high-leverage.
2. **The 10% take is the floor.** Every revenue line that bypasses Whatnot (Brand Deals, NeoWorld University subscription) keeps 100% of the gross. That's why the growth thesis leans on University + Brand Deals, not just more stream hours.
3. **Auction format means margin is partly market-driven.** Demand determines final sale price. Buying low and streaming during peak viewer hours is how the team optimizes within a format the team can't fully control.
