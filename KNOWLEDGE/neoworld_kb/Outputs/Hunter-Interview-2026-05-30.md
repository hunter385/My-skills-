### Q9: Hunter (COO) vs. Laura (Ops Manager + CFO) — role split
- **No rigid line.** They partner on Cycle Rocks. Projects get assigned by best-fit rather than by title.
- Operating model is collaborative, not hierarchical between them.

### Q10: Cycle cadence — three different rhythms for three different contexts

This is the most important strategic insight from Pass 2:

| Context | Cycle length |
|---------|--------------|
| **Ready Set Grow (Hunter's internal business)** | **6-week cycles** |
| **Ready Set Grow (what RSG teaches churches)** | **90-day cycles** |
| **NeoWorld (Hunter's COO business)** | **Monthly cycles** |

Each business calibrates the Middle Method to its own pace:
- Pastoral church teams need longer cycles (90 days) because change in a church culture is slower.
- RSG-the-business needs faster cycles (6 weeks) because it's a content business that needs to iterate fast.
- NeoWorld needs the fastest cycles (monthly) because it's a streaming business where the market and inventory turn over quickly.

The Middle Method framework is cadence-agnostic. The principle stays — Cycles fight distraction, Cooldowns fight misalignment — but the duration scales with the speed of the business.
