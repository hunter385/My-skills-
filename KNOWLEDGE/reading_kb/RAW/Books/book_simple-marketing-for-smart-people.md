# WHAT WOULD MY CUSTOMER NEED TO BELIEVE BEFORE THEY BUY?
It's removing every obstacle or objection they have before they ask. This is belief building.

What's the one thing I need to do that makes everything else easier
1. Identify your prospects current beliefs
2. Identify the beliefs required for the purchase to be obvious
3. Create the marketing content that builds those required beliefs

Tiago has over a hundred beliefs that he thinks is a requirement to plug if people are going to enjoy his content.
Every time Tiago goes on a podcast or makes content himself, he's trying to plug one of these holes.
"Instill the beliefs needed for Customers to buy."

Think of the 5 whys. And you also have to take that into effect when you think about beliefs, that every belief has a link to a deeper belief.
- Don't use the 5 whys - it can make people feel judgmental.
- Ask, "What were you trying to accomplish?"
- How did you go about making that decision?
- Ask "the last time this problem occurred, How did you deal with it?"

In short, it's about how aware your audience is of their problem and your solution, which changes how you should speak to them. The common breakdown looks like this:
1. Unaware – They don't realize they have a problem or need. Message: Grab attention with curiosity or an emotional hook before introducing the issue.
2. Problem Aware – They know they have a problem, but don't know what solutions exist. Message: Agitate the pain and clarify the real problem.
3. Solution Aware – They know solutions exist, but don't know about yours. Message: Show how your solution works and why it's different.
4. Product Aware – They know your product but haven't decided to buy. Message: Build trust, show proof, offer incentives.
5. Most Aware – They know and trust you; they just need a nudge. Message: Clear call-to-action, urgency, or exclusivity.

There are very few people who hold the beliefs required to purchase your product. You have to train them.
- Who cares about a 15% discount if I don't believe I need coaching?

The chain of beliefs goes from "totally unaware" of you and your product to "fully informed."

# Anchor beliefs to the customer journey.
Awareness, consideration, decision.

### Awareness.
Customers realize there's a problem.
The customer is looking for businesses that understand them and their problem.
They can't really articulate what their problem is, so you need to help them articulate it. Give them the words to better describe their problem.

## Consideration.
The customer is exploring or considering different solutions.
You need to "toss your hat in the ring" and explore if this is the best viable solution.
The beliefs that we instill should invalidate the other options. We should be the only one left, a category of one.

## Decision.
This is the transition from marketing to selling.
If you've done your marketing correctly, this should be really easy.
It's essential that you reinforce trustworthiness and assure the customer they're making the right decision.
