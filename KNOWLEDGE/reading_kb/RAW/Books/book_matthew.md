### Author and Audience
The Gospel of Matthew was written by **Matthew** (also called Levi), a former tax collector who became one of Jesus' twelve apostles. This Gospel was primarily written for a **Jewish** audience, aiming to demonstrate that Jesus is the promised Messiah foretold in the Hebrew Scriptures.

### Historical Context
Matthew was likely written between 70-90 AD, after the destruction of the Jerusalem temple. The Jewish people were scattered and struggling with their identity. Matthew addresses these concerns by showing how Jesus inaugurates a new covenant while fulfilling the old one.

### Old Testament Background
Matthew contains more quotations from and allusions to the Old Testament than any other Gospel. Familiarity with Jewish Scriptures, particularly the books of Isaiah, Jeremiah, and the Psalms, will greatly enhance your understanding. Matthew frequently uses the phrase "This was to fulfill what was spoken..." to connect Jesus' life with prophetic writings.
