## THE BIG AIM
a clear sense of how to live confidently in a personal walk that is complemented by an ongoing conversational relationship with God.

This statement captures the essence of spiritual maturity:
1. living with confidence
2. that comes from constant connection with God.

**What "living confidently" means:**
Confidence here isn't arrogance or self-sufficiency. It's the settled assurance that comes from knowing God is actively with you and speaking to you. You're not constantly second-guessing yourself or paralyzed by fear of making wrong decisions. Instead, you move forward knowing you're in conversation with someone who guides you.

**What "personal walk" means:**
This is your unique journey with God—not someone else's formula or a copied spiritual life. God relates to you specifically, considering your personality, circumstances, and calling. Your walk looks different from others' because God is developing a relationship with *you*, not producing identical spiritual clones.

**What "complemented by" means:**
The conversational relationship isn't an add-on or optional upgrade—it's the foundation that makes confident living possible. The conversation doesn't replace action; it informs and empowers it. You're not waiting for a voice from heaven before every step, but you're also not operating independently. The two work together.

**What "ongoing conversational relationship" means:**
This directly connects to the vision statement on this page: "We are meant to live in ongoing conversation with God—speaking and being spoken to." It's not occasional check-ins or crisis prayers. It's a continuous back-and-forth where God's voice is developing you into "an intelligent, freely cooperative relationship"—not mechanical obedience, but friendship and collaborative partnership.

## THE IDEAL SCENARIO
> We are meant to live in ongoing conversation with God—speaking and being spoken to.

## WHY DOES GOD WANT TO TALK WITH US?
He is not simply creating obedient followers who mechanically carry out commands. Instead, God is intentionally developing mature, spiritually formed friends—people who actively participate alongside him in meaningful work and collaborative partnership.

## GOD MADE YOU FOR FRIENDSHIP AND COMPANIONSHIP

**THE CORE IDEA**
Prayer isn't primarily a tool for getting answers or figuring out what to do next.
It's the language of a friendship.
When you hear God, you're not just receiving instructions—you're experiencing his presence with you.

**WHY THIS MATTERS**
If you approach prayer mainly looking for direction ("What should I do?"), you've reduced God to a cosmic GPS. But Dallas Willard is saying God wants something deeper—he wants *communion*, which means sharing life together, talking about what's happening, being together.
This connects directly to the foundation he lays out: "God has created us for intimate friendship with himself" and the relationship model where "abiding involves conscious communication".

**PRACTICALLY**
Instead of starting prayer with "What should I do today?" start with "Immanuel, God with us" and simply relate—talk about what's interesting, what's hard, what you're noticing. The guidance will come naturally out of that friendship, not as a replacement for it.

**PRACTICE**
- Begin prayer by *relating* not requesting.
- Talk about what is happening, what is interesting, what is sad.
- Talk about other things besides what God wants done today.

**WHAT IT PREVENTS**
It keeps you from treating God as a "cosmic boss" or a "taskmaster"
- Terms describing an unhealthy view of God as primarily concerned with issuing orders and monitoring compliance, rather than seeking friendship and companionship with believers.
- This false image of God prevents people from experiencing prayer as conversational relationship and reduces spiritual life to duty-driven performance.

## THE PRAYER PARADOX
Many Christians believe hearing God matters, but don't know how it works

**WHY THIS MATTERS:**
We need to solve this puzzle: help believers clearly understand how to hear God's voice and give them practical ways to do it with confidence.
To do this, we must treat hearing God as learnable—not spooky, not rare, not reserved for elites.

## 3 THINGS THAT KEEP US FROM HEARING GOD
1. **Not knowing God speaks in many forms** — Do not reduce hearing God to only one channel.
2. **Wrong motives** — Many people only want to hear from God so they can feel safe, comfortable, or like they're doing the right thing. They're treating God like a spiritual security system instead of wanting an actual relationship with him. In other words: they're using prayer to get what they want (protection, validation, confirmation they're good), rather than to know and love God himself.
3. **Our ability to hear God is shaped by what we think God is like.** If you have the wrong idea about what God is really like and what he wants for you, it becomes nearly impossible to understand how he communicates with you. This wrong view of God also messes up how you understand the church and how spiritual authority is supposed to work. At the root of it: all human problems start with thinking wrongly about God, which then leads us to think wrongly about ourselves. In other words: Your picture of God shapes everything—how you hear him, how you see the church, and how you see yourself.

## COMMON MISTAKES PEOPLE MAKE
**THE CORE IDEA**
This section identifies three common mistakes people make when thinking about hearing from God. Each mistake comes from a distorted understanding of how God's guidance actually works, and each leads to either passivity, anxiety, or spiritual manipulation.

**WHY THIS MATTERS**
These aren't just theoretical errors—they're practical traps that keep people from experiencing the conversational relationship God wants. The first mistake (needing constant direction) turns you into a spiritual anxious wreck. The second mistake (Bible-only guidance) ignores that God still speaks personally. The third mistake (everything that happens is God's will) removes human responsibility and makes you passive.

| Mistake | Warning Signs | What to Do Instead |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **"I need to hear from God every minute"** | This is very harmful and will quickly lead to disaster. | If you feel pressure to get instructions from God every minute, this is not healthy. |
| **"Everything I need is in the Bible"** | This is seriously misguided and very harmful. The Bible gives us the principles we need, but we still have to apply them. | The Bible is our foundation, but you still need to learn how God guides you personally within those principles. |
| **"Everything that happens is God's will"** | Many things that happen are not God's will. God wants us to make decisions about many events. | Don't confuse what happens with what God wants. God's guidance happens through conversation with him. |
