# ONE BIG THING
**Hurry is not a scheduling problem.**
**Hurry is a soul problem.**

The core insight of the book is not "slow down."
It is **remove whatever prevents you from living at the pace of love**.
Everything else connects back to this.

---
# THE BIG PICTURE
Hurry:
- Fragments attention
- Erodes love
- Kills depth
- Makes formation impossible

Unhurried living:
- Creates space for God
- Restores presence
- Enables obedience
- Produces lasting fruit

This book is ultimately about **spiritual formation through pace**.
---
# THE BACKSTORY
Modern life has normalized:
- Speed as virtue
- Busyness as worth
- Exhaustion as success

The result:
- High activity
- Low depth
- Strong opinions
- Weak interior lives

Hurry becomes invisible because it feels productive.
---
# CATCH UP QUICK
John Mark Comer's argument in one line:
> You cannot be spiritually healthy in a chronically hurried life.
The solution is not better time management.
The solution is **a Rule of Life shaped around Jesus' pace**.
---
# ZOOM OUT
## Hurry as the Enemy
- Hurry crowds out prayer
- Hurry resists silence
- Hurry avoids dependence
- Hurry numbs desire for God
## Speed vs Love
- Love requires presence
- Presence requires margin
- Margin requires limits
You cannot love people you are rushing through.
# ZOOM IN
## False Solutions
## More Time Will Not Save You
- Efficiency increases demand
- Time expands to fill expectations
- More capacity attracts more responsibility
**Reframe**
The problem is not lack of time.
The problem is lack of **constraint**.
## Hustle Spirituality
- Doing things for God
- Without being with God
- In God's name
- At a pace God never asked for
**This links directly to pastors and business owners.**

# THE SOLUTION
## The Easy Yoke
Jesus' invitation:
> Come to me… my yoke is easy, my burden is light.
This implies:
- A shared load
- A shared pace
- A different way of working

**Key shift**
From maximizing output To abiding and producing fruit
---
# HOW IT WORKS
## A Rule of Life
A Rule of Life is:
- A trellis, not a cage
- Intentional constraints
- Designed to protect love
It answers one question:
> **What pace of life allows me to stay emotionally healthy and spiritually alive?**
---
# THE FOUR PRACTICES
## Silence and Solitude
### WHY IT MATTERS
- Exposes hurry
- Surfaces anxiety
- Reveals dependence issues
### WHAT IT COSTS
- Control
- Distraction
- Self-importance
### PRACTICAL APPLICATION
- Start with minutes, not hours
- Schedule silence like a meeting
- Expect discomfort before clarity
---
## Sabbath
### WHY IT MATTERS
- Declares God as provider
- Breaks productivity addiction
- Restores delight
### SABBATH IS
- Stopping
- Resting
- Delighting
- Worshiping
### SABBATH IS NOT
- A day off
- Catch-up time
- Passive recovery
---
## Simplicity
### WHY IT MATTERS
- Complexity creates hurry
- Excess creates anxiety
- Too much choice fragments desire
### SIMPLICITY LOOKS LIKE
- Fewer commitments
- Fewer inputs
- Fewer ambitions, but deeper ones
---
## Slowing
### WHY IT MATTERS
- Re-trains the nervous system
- Makes room for awareness
- Aligns body and soul
### PRACTICES
- Drive slower
- Walk more
- Single-task
- Arrive early
