# Questions
1. What is your own impossible goal?
2. How will you utilize time as a tool?
3. What does your impossible goal and timeline do to your floor?
4. What will you let go of that's keeping you where you're now at?
5. What is your own unique pathway to your impossible goal?
6. Who will need to get involved that can make your mission possible?
7. Will you stagnate or will you scale?

# BLUF

### Frame
*Where are you going and how soon?*

### Floor
*What will you no longer tolerate?*

---

### Frame
**Frame** is about the *lens you see through*. Your goals set the frame — they filter what matters and what doesn't.
- **Impossible goal (#1)**: Choosing a goal so big it forces you to redefine what's possible. This stretches your vision and forces innovation.
- **Time as a tool (#2)**: Compressing the timeline makes the frame sharper. A shorter deadline forces urgency, eliminates false requirements, and clarifies what's essential.
Together, "impossible + urgent" sets the frame that defines your strategy.

---
### Floor
**Floor** is about *what you refuse to tolerate anymore*. It's the baseline you won't go below.
- **Raising your standards (#3)**: Your impossible goal demands a higher minimum standard. You can't afford mediocrity, excuses, or inconsistencies.
- **Letting go (#4)**: To raise the floor, you must strip out what no longer fits — bad clients, weak team members, distracting projects, or old habits.
Frame pulls you upward, but Floor keeps you from slipping back into noise and complexity.

---
### Focus
**Focus** is about *choosing the single viable path forward*. It's filtering for the highest-leverage actions and relationships.
- **Pathway (#5)**: The unique route that only you (or your business) can take to hit the impossible goal. It's simple, not scattered.
- **Partners (#6)**: You can't scale alone. Scaling requires "Super Whos" — people or partners who 10x your capacity and accelerate the chosen pathway.
Focus is the execution engine: you commit to one path, with the right allies, and cut everything else.
