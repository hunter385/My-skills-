- That agreement includes five distinct stages:
  The Connection: This is the first part of the sales process, but you may think of it as what happens before the sale.   The Deliverable: This is when the vendor (still you) completes the work or delivers the goods as promised to the client, within the time parameters and the “work standards” agreed upon.
  The Collection: In this stage, the client pays the vendor (yay, you!) for the work or goods provided, within the time parameters and amounts agreed upon.
  The Conclusion: This is when all agreed-upon terms are delivered and both parties confirm that all the parameters of the agreement are complete. This may also be when new terms are reached and the process starts again.
