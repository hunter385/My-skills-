## Marketing is the act of *creating* then *relieving* tension.
- Helping human beings find what they are looking for
- If you see a way to make things better, you have a marketing problem… You have to get the word out.

## Marketing is the generous act of helping someone solve a problem. Their problem.
- Marketer is to meant to serve
  - The best marketing isn't selfish
    - Marketings is the generous act of seeking to help people become who they want to become
    - Opportunities to solve their problems.
- Marketing is a billion tiny whispers
- Marketing is about helping people solve a problem not "getting the word out" and disrupting or spamming them in what they were doing
  - Understand their worldview and desires
    - ...And be missed when you're gone

## The Lock and Key
- It doesn't make sense to make a key and then run around and try to find a lock to open.
  - The only productive solution is to find a lock and then fashion a key.
    - It's easier to make products and services for the customers you're seeking to serve, than find customers for your products and services.

## Marketing in 5 steps
1. Invent a thing worth making — And a contribution worth talking about
2. Design it in a way where a few people will benefit from and care about
3. Tell a story that matches the built in narrative and dreams of that tiny group of people — With a story worth telling — The smallest viable market
4. Spread the word
5. Show up — Consistently. Generously for years and years…to organize and build in the confidence in the change you seek to make — To earn permission to follow up — To earn enrollment to teach

This is marketing: ideas that spread win.
- Marketers don't use consumers to solve their company's problems …. they use marketing to solve the customers problems
  - Consumers don't want what the marketer wants
  - Don't believe what they believe
  - Don't care what they care about

## At the heart of our culture is our belief in status.
- We are looking for things that take us to where we're going next
- Status seeking
- They are seeking for "where to go and how to get there."
  - Persistent, Consistent, and frequent stories, delivered to an aligned audience, will earn attention, then trust, and finally action (CTA)

Make the right thing for the right people.

## Things Marketers Know
1. A small group of committed creative people can change the world… In fact, they are the only ones who do.
2. You can not change everyone. Therefore, asking who is this for can focus your actions / deal with non believers
3. Humans tell each other stories.. it's the only way this is going to work to persuade them
4. What you say isn't nearly as important as what others say about you.

## INDIA GLASSES STORY
- Many of the people needed glasses
- Only a 1/3 would buy them?
- Why?
- Seth knew there was a pain there and this was relief
- "If it was me… I would totally get a pair"
- But nevertheless, they weren't selling like they wished.
  - However, this isn't how consumers think….
  - They don't think or believe the way you do.
  - You have to be empathetic enough or ask to help you know what their thinking

## People don't want what you make… they want what it will do for them.
- They want the way it will make them feel.
- And there aren't that many feelings to choose from.
  - Most marketers deliver the same feelings — We just do it in different ways and services and products and stories — And we do it for different people in different moments
  - You can bring someone: Belonging, Connection, Peace of mind, Status, Or one of the other most desired emotions
- The thing you sell is a road to achieve those emotions
  - We let everyone down when we focus on the *tactics* not the *outcomes*
  - Who's it for / What's it for

### Stories | Connections | Experiences
- **STORIES** — Tell stories that are true because we made them true by using our actions, products and services.
- **CONNECTIONS** — Make connections. Humans are lonely. They want to be seen and known. They want to be a part of something.
- **EXPERIENCES** — We Create experiences. Using a product, giving a donation, calling customer service. Each of these actions is a part of our customers *story* with us. Each builds a little bit towards our *connection*. Create these *stories* with intent. The entire organization works for and with the marketers. Marketing is all of it: What we make, How we make it, Who we make it for.

## Be Market-Driven not Marketing Driven
- When you're *marketing driven* — You're focused on the newest Facebook data hacks, your new logo, and your Canadian pricing model
- When you're *market driven* — You think a lot about the hopes and dreams of your customers and their friends, you listen to their frustrations, and invest in changing the culture

### THE SMALLEST VIABLE MARKET
- **WHAT CHANGE ARE YOU TRYING TO MAKE?** Start small. Don't do something impossible and grandiose. Something we can actually hurdle and leap. And based off of that success, stretch that out a little bigger next time on even bigger challenges.
- **WHAT PROMISE ARE YOU MAKING?** Anytime a marketer shows up on the scene they are making a promise… "If you do X you'll get Y". All effective markers make a promise. Your promise is directly connected to the change you seek to make… and it's addressed to the people you seek to change.
- **Who are you seeking to change?** The world is too big for you to reach everyone. Who is it for? You need to reach someone or a group of someone's. Do they share a Belief, Geography, Demographic, Or a psychographic.

### How do we begin choosing?
- **Worldviews** — Start with what they dream of, believe and want…
- **Personas** — Bargain Bill, Hurried Henry, Careful Carla

If we are agreeing that we are here to teach our customers something to make a change happen ... let's transform our thinking from prospects to calling them our *students*. Where are our students? What will they benefit from learning? Are they open to being taught? What will they tell the others?

- If you had a chance to teach them… what will you want to teach?
- If you had a chance to learn… what would you like to be taught?

**Coloring the ocean purple** — A little bit of purple dye colors a pool. Drop it in the ocean… no one would notice. The key here is… you can have the extraordinary product in the world… but if you drop it in the ocean… nothing happens. That doesn't mean you give up hope.. you just have to find a large swimming pool… that's enough to make a difference. Begin there, with obsessive focus. Once it works… find another swimming pool… even better, let your customers spread your idea.

### The Simple Marketing Promise
- My product is for people who believe ______
- I will focus on people who want _____
- I promise that engaging with my product will help you get _____

## Better isn't up to you
- There are over 250 models of motorcycles in Cleveland. Can you name them? No. Same is true for ketchup, churches, and insurance. So what do we do if we are interested in the topic… we remember the *best one*.
- Best for what? — Best for our target customer.
- Some things are linear (ruler > thumb > peppercorn). Some are subjective (best restaurant). Who is our target and what are the categories they have in mind? We want to be a signal flare for what our target customer was looking for.

### X, Y AXIS for extremes
Choose two from: Speed, Price, Performance, Ingredients, Purity, Sustainability, Obviousness, Maintenance cost, Safety, Edginess, Distribution, Network Effect, Imminence, Visibility, Trendiness, Privacy, Professionalism, Difficulty, Elitism, Danger, Experimental, Limited, Incomplete. If you choose the most popular attributes, you'll be in a crowded space. Instead, find 2 axis that may have been overlooked — you can be the clear and obvious choice.

### Which brand makes you feel this emotion?
Safe / Beautiful / Powerful / Worthy / Responsible / Smart / Connected / Hip

### BEYOND COMMODITIES
- Problem First — Marketers don't begin with a clever solution… instead we start with a group we seek to serve with a problem they seek to solve and a change they seek to make.
- Does it work? — Quality is expected, but it doesn't make you different.
- When you know what you stand for, you don't need to compete: if you merely try to fill a hole in the market, we're doomed to eventually become a commodity.

### THE CANVAS OF DREAMS AND DESIRES
What do people want? A $20 bill is meaningless… it's what you can buy with them. When we are marketing change… you're offering a new emotional state…. a step closer to their dreams and greatest desires. We sell feelings, status, and connection… not tasks or stuff.

Core basket of dreams and desires: Adventure, Affection, Avoiding new things, Belonging, Community, Control, Creativity, Delight, Freedom of expression, Freedom of movement, Friendship, Good looks, Health, Learning new things, Luxury, Nostalgia, Obedience, Participation, Peace of mind, Physical activity, Power, Reassurance, Reliability, Respect, Revenge, Romance, Safety, Security, Sex, Strength, Sympathy, Tension.

*Innovative marketers invent new solutions that work with old emotions. (Capitalize on nostalgia)*

**Where's the angry bear?** — If we have a pretty good grasp on the dreams of the customer… but they are not acting like we thought they would… they have analysis paralysis. What are people afraid of that is causing them to be stuck in their fear?

### MORE OF THE WHO: the smallest viable market
**The Virtuous Cycle & Network Effects** — Every very good customer, gets you another one. Your best customers become your new sales people. Build something that gets *better* when it gets spread.

**A Thousand True Fans** — Someone who will buy anything you produce. Build this first. Matter to a few first.

**Don't have Taylor Swift as a Role Model** — For the very few they are a hit machine. For the rest of us… build the 1000 loyal fans… smallest viable market… and build out from there. A path of connection, empathy and change.

**ALL CRITICS ARE RIGHT (All Critics are wrong)** — There are at least 2 audiences that connect with our product: the desired customers, and the accidental audience. When we seek feedback we are asking to be proven wrong. Instead, seek advice: "What advice do you have on how I can make it more fit your worldview more closely?"

**WHY DON'T PEOPLE CHOOSE YOU?** — Fill in the sentence: "For people that want what I want & believe what I believe…. their choice of ______ is exactly correct."

### DEEP CHANGE is DIFFICULT, and WORTH IT
People will take action or not take action that reinforces their internal narratives. People are most likely to change if it means it will change the perception of their status, affiliation, and dominance.

**GOLDEN PERCEPTIVE QUESTION:** *Do people like me do things like this?*

**The Internal Narrative** — We don't make decisions in a vacuum. Instead we base them off of the perception of our cohort. We make decisions based around "is this what a successful version of myself would do/wear/buy?"

Normalization creates culture. Marketers normalize new behaviors.

### People like US do things like this.
- ELITE — Elite is in high standing, but can be taken away from us.
- EXCLUSIVE — Exclusive is in your control. Members are part of "US"... walk away and you are a part of "THEM".

### Trust and Tension Create Forward Motion
- **Pattern Match** — add this pattern into your already built routine
- **Pattern Interrupt** — requires undoing before you can earn motion
- *When life interferes, new patterns are established* — profitable to market to new dads, engaged women, recently moved people

**Tension Can Change Patterns** — If you are going to market a *Pattern Interrupt* it is going to require you to create the tension that can only be released by being willing to change an engrained pattern.

**Create FOMO tension** for people not doing your new thing. **Tension is Not the Same as Fear.** Marketers Create Tension, and *Forward Motion* Relieves that Tension. The tension comes from *status seeking*. Create Tension. Then Relieve it.

### STATUS, DOMINANCE, AND AFFILIATION
The desire to change our status or to protect it.... drives almost everything we do. Status is not the same as wealth.

**Six things about status**
- Status is always relative
- Status is in the eyes of the beholder
- Status attended to is the status that matters
- Status has inertia
- Status is learned
- Shame is the status killer

*Are People Like Us Doing Things Like This?* — Affiliation: Who is standing next to me?

### A Better Business Plan: Modern Business Plan
- **Truth** — Describes the world how it is now. Needs in target audience, competitors, tech standards, how others have succeeded/failed.
- **Assertions** — Describes how you are going to change things. What you'll do, what impact it will have.
- **Alternatives** — What you do if you miss budgets, deadlines, sales.
- **People** — Who is on our team, who we are serving, what they believe about status.
- **Money** — How much you need, how you'll spend it, cashflow.

### People Like Us Talk like This — Logo, Type, Photos, Aesthetics
**Does Your Logo Matter?** — Pick your 5 favorite logos. You probably picked logos because of the Brand's Promise more than the pixel placement.

### TREAT DIFFERENT PEOPLE DIFFERENTLY
- Innovation early adopters want: Being first, the risk of it not working
- The middle majority: Be in sync, "people like me do things like this"

Nobody wants to feel *dumb*. They need the "you can't fail at this" stack.

### REACHING THE RIGHT PEOPLE
**Goals, Strategy, Tactics**
- **Goals** — the change you seek to make
- **Strategy** — the umbrella under your tactics (e.g. earn trust and attention, create alliances and partnerships)
- **Tactics** — dozens or hundreds of steps to support your strategy

**Coke** — Goal: more people drink more coke. Strategy: run hundreds of ads. Tactic: the content of the ad.
**Patagonia** — Goal: get outdoor enthusiasts to care about environment via Patagonia apparel. Strategy: redefine environmental impact and clothing quality, give a label and tool to evangelize. Tactic: recycling, brick stores, materials, pricing. Same strategy 30 years.

### Advertising is a special case, an optional engine for growth
- An ad unnoticed doesn't exist. Hard to find an ad that works. If you do, scale it precisely. Create tension in the ad. Cliffhanger.
- Ads are unearned media… and the people who view it know that.

**A simple guide to online direct marketing**
1. The ad exists to get a click
2. The click exists to get a sale or earn permission
3. The sale exists to lead to other sales or to word of mouth
4. Permission exists to lead to education and to get a sale
5. That's it

Every step has a cost. Assign values to each step. If you can't measure it, it doesn't count.

**FREQUENCY** — We remember what we *rehearse* — things we see again and again. The familiar is normal and the normal is trusted. Don't change your ads when you get bored. Frequency builds trust.

### PRICE IS A STORY
Pricing is a marketing tool, not simply a way to get money. People form assumptions and associations based on your pricing. Your pricing should align to the extremes you claim in your marketing.

"Cheap" is another way of saying "scared". Racing to be the cheapest, you're not promising change. Low price is the last refuge of a marketer whose run out of generous ideas.

**And what about Free?** — Have free ideas that spread. Then expensive expressions of those ideas worth paying for (song free, concert ticket $84).

**Trust and Risk, Trust and Expense** — Higher cost = more trust needed. Build trust. Add Value. Lowering price does NOT make you more trusted. It does the opposite.

### PERMISSION AND REMARKABILITY IN A VIRTUOUS CYCLE
**Permission is Anticipated, Personal, and Relevant** — Spam doesn't scale. Earn the right for permission. They get your message in the medium they want to receive it.

**Earn your own permission and own it** — Bring them to your turf. Get rid of the middle man.

**Show up with generosity** — How do you connect with people who want to connect with you?

*What will people like us talk about next year?*

**Design for Evangelism** — Design evangelism into the fabric of what you are creating. People won't evangelize because it's important to you… they'll do it if it's important to them.

**Trust is as Scarce as Attention**
**Famous to the Tribe** — Your goal is not to grow social media numbers. Be famous to your smallest viable audience. 3000 loyal die-hard fans is plenty.

### THE FUNNEL
**Trust isn't static** — Top: pour attention. Bottom: committed loyal customers. In between: most slip out.

**You can fix your funnel**
1. Make sure the right people are attracted
2. Make sure the promise aligns with where you want them to go
3. Remove steps
4. Support those you're engaging with — reinforce dreams, diminish fears
5. Use tension to create forward motion
6. Hand fans a megaphone

**Funnel math** — Lifetime customer value first. Cost per click - cost earned = net profit.

**Where's your bridge?** — Connective tribes work better together (network effect). What will I tell my friend? Why will I tell my friend? It has to be because I want to.
