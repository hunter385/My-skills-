The Danger of “Not as Important” Projects ([Location 482](https://readwise.io/to_kindle?action=open&asin=B000OIZUP8&location=482))

<empty-block/>
People in my seminars often comment on why I recommend that they make a complete projects list, with no distinctions about priorities, time frame, or size. The distinction I do recommend is very simple: projects versus someday/maybe. The first list is a complete inventory of all the open loops we’ve created that require more than one action to close them. Most people have thirty to a hundred of those. The second list is all those projects that you might want to do sometime in the future but have no commitment to move on them right now. If it’s on the projects list, you need to decide next actions equally on each one and review the status of each regularly.
