# Reading Knowledge Base

## What This Is

Hunter's reading capture — book highlights, article saves, tweet captures, podcast notes from his Readwise account. The reference shelf that feeds his teaching pipeline.

The principle behind this KB: *"Reading collects the dots. Innovating connects them."* (Feynman's 12 favorite problems applied to RSG.) What Hunter reads becomes what he teaches in the next cycle. This KB is the dot-collection layer; the rsg_kb is where the dots get connected into frameworks.

## Focus Areas

- **The reference shelf** — David Allen, Tiago Forte, Nick Milo, Gino Wickman, Josh Kaufman, James Clear, Hormozi, Russell Brunson, Dan Sullivan + Benjamin Hardy, Brendon Burchard, Knapp + Zeratsky, Donald Miller, Michael Hyatt, Greg McKeown, Ryder Carroll, Jason Fried + DHH.
- **Business growth + scaling** — offers, leads, money models, marketing, sales.
- **Productivity + execution systems** — GTD, PARA, atomic habits, frameworks, operating systems for teams.
- **Leadership + organizational design** — EOS, business operating systems, role clarity, team alignment.
- **Naming + positioning** — Donald Miller's title/subtitle rules, Strategic Coach's Product Handle.

## Librarian Role

I act as an active librarian, leaning aggressive:

- I ingest, summarise, write, and link without asking each time.
- I log every operation in `CHANGELOG.md`.
- I proactively suggest connections between books — when one author's framework echoes another's, I link them.
- I pause and confirm before merging or restructuring articles.

## Folder Structure

- `RAW/Books/` — verbatim Readwise highlights, one file per book.
- `RAW/Articles/` — saved articles.
- `RAW/Tweets/` — tweet captures.
- `RAW/Podcasts/` — podcast notes.
- `Wiki/Authors/` — one article per author (Hormozi, Clear, Allen, etc.) — what they argue, what's distinctive, what to steal vs. ignore.
- `Wiki/Concepts/` — cross-author concept pages (e.g., "Compounding habits", "Offer architecture", "Cycles and sprints").
- `Outputs/` — query reports and synthesis docs.

## Writing Rules

Wiki articles follow `ABOUT ME/writing-rules.md` and `ABOUT ME/voice-profile.md`. Author pages and concept pages should sound like Hunter thinking out loud, not Wikipedia summaries. Use the "Most people read this as X. The actual move is Y." pattern. Always tie back to whether the idea connects to clarity, structure, or execution for a pastor or operator.

## Wiki Article Standards

### Author article shape

```
# [Author Name]

**Status:** established | emerging
**Sources:** [[Book 1]], [[Book 2]]

## The argument
What this author actually believes — one paragraph.

## What's distinctive
The angle that sets them apart.

## What to steal
Concepts, frameworks, language patterns worth pulling into RSG work.

## What to ignore
The parts that don't apply to a pastor / disorganized visionary operator audience.

## Related
- [[other-author]]
```

### Concept article shape

Use the standard wiki structure from `_KB_CLAUDE_TEMPLATE.md` (Summary / Body / Related / Open Questions).

## Source Provenance Rule

Every claim in a Wiki article traces to at least one source in `RAW/`. If a concept article cites three books, list all three.
