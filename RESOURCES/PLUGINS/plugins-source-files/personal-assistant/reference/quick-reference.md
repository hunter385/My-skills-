# Personal Assistant — Quick Reference

## How It Works

Just talk. Tell me about your day — calls, tasks, meetings, ideas, frustrations, decisions — and I'll organise everything behind the scenes. You never need to think about where things are stored.

## Slash Commands

| Command | What it does |
|---------|-------------|
| `/log` | Quick captain's log entry — capture what's happening right now |
| `/tasks` | Show your current open tasks, grouped by urgency |
| `/briefing` | Run your morning briefing on demand |
| `/eod` | Run your end-of-day summary with optional reflection |
| `/tidy` | Scan Downloads or Desktop and clean up clutter |

## What Happens Automatically

**Task extraction** — When you mention something you need to do ("I need to send her the proposal by Friday"), it becomes a tracked task. You don't need to say "create a task."

**Contact tracking** — When you mention people with context ("had a call with Bhav about the course launch"), their details are saved. Ask me "when did I last talk to Bhav?" anytime.

**Preference capture** — When you state a preference ("I prefer morning meetings") or make a decision ("going with Teachable for the course"), it's logged and will eventually be promoted to your About Me files.

**Output tracking** — Every file I create for you is logged so your end-of-day summary can show what you produced.

## Scheduled Tasks

**Morning briefing** — Your daily briefing runs automatically. It covers: what's on today, overdue tasks, what you're waiting on, and yesterday's highlights.

**End-of-day summary** — Your EOD runs automatically. It covers: what got done, what you created, what's still open, and tomorrow's preview. Includes an optional reflection prompt.

## Importing Existing Data

You don't have to start from zero. There are several ways to bring in what you already have:

**Contacts:** Drop a CSV or Excel file of your contacts into the chat and ask me to import them. I can handle most common formats. You can also export from Google Contacts, your phone, or any CRM.

**Tasks:** If you use Notion for tasks, connect the Notion connector and point me to your task database. I'll pull everything in and link tasks to your work areas. For other tools, export to CSV and drop it in.

**You can do this anytime** — during setup or weeks later when you realise you want your existing data in the system.

## Tips

- **Just start talking.** You don't need to use /log every time. If you're in a session and start chatting about your day, I'll capture it.
- **Be specific about dates.** "By Friday" is better than "soon" — it lets me track and remind.
- **Mention people by name.** "Called Sarah about the timeline" gives me more to work with than "had a call."
- **Use /tasks regularly.** It's your at-a-glance view of everything on your plate.
- **Tasks link to areas.** Every task connects to a work area or project, so "show me Marketing tasks" just works.
- **Do the EOD reflection.** Even two sentences help. The patterns that emerge over time are genuinely useful.
