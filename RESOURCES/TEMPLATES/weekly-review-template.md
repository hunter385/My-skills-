# Weekly review template

Use this template for a structured end-of-week review. It pulls together what happened, what moved forward, and what needs attention next week. You can do this manually by filling in the sections, or ask Claude to compile it from your calendar, email, tasks, and memory.

---

## The review

### Week of [date range]

### What got done

[List the meaningful things you completed this week. Not every small task — the things that moved projects forward or created real value.]

- [Accomplishment with context]
- [Accomplishment with context]
- [Accomplishment with context]

### What didn't get done (and why)

[Things you planned to do but didn't. Be honest about why. Ran out of time? Blocked by someone? Avoided it? The "why" matters more than the list.]

- [Task] — [Why it didn't happen]
- [Task] — [Why it didn't happen]

### Key decisions made

[Any significant decisions this week. What did you decide and why? This is useful context for future-you.]

- [Decision and reasoning]

### What I learned

[Anything worth remembering. A new tool, a process that worked, something that didn't work, a conversation that shifted your thinking.]

- [Lesson or insight]

### Open questions or blockers

[Things you're stuck on, waiting on, or need to think through. Getting these out of your head and onto paper is half the point of the review.]

- [Question or blocker]

## How to use this

**Option 1: Auto-compile with Claude.** Say: "Run my weekly review." If you have Gmail, Google Calendar, and Notion connected, Claude can pull your sent emails, calendar events, and completed tasks to draft the review. You review and refine it.

**Option 2: Fill it in yourself.** Spend 15-20 minutes on Friday afternoon filling this in. The act of reflection is the point — even without connected tools.

**Option 3: Set it up as a scheduled task.** Ask Claude to create a Friday afternoon scheduled task that runs this review automatically. It drafts it from your activity; you approve it and add anything it missed. The Cookbook has a recipe for this in Tier 2.

---

## Notes

- The "what didn't get done" section is the most valuable. Patterns here reveal systemic issues — not failures, but signals about where your system needs adjusting.
- Keep it under one page. If your review is three pages long, you're overcomplicating it.
- Save reviews to the relevant project's `outputs/` folder (or `WORK AREAS/Admin-PA/` for general reviews) with the naming convention: `Weekly-Review_[date].md`
- Over time, these reviews feed the System Review skill. It reads your memory and weekly reviews to spot patterns and propose improvements. That's the feedback loop in action.
