# Decision document template

Use this template when you're facing a decision that's worth thinking through properly — not every small choice, but the ones where getting it wrong would cost real time, money, or momentum. Hiring, pricing changes, tool switches, project direction, partnerships.

---

## The decision

### What's the question?

[State the decision as a clear question. "Should we switch from Mailchimp to ConvertKit?" is better than "Email platform review."]

### Why does this matter?

[What's at stake? What happens if you get it wrong? What's the cost of doing nothing? One or two sentences that ground the decision in reality.]

### When do you need to decide by?

[Is there a deadline? If not, what's the cost of waiting? Some decisions get easier with time. Others get harder.]

---

## Options

### Option A: [Name it]

**What this means:** [One sentence describing the option]

**Arguments for:**
- [Specific reason with evidence if available]
- [Another reason]

**Arguments against:**
- [Specific risk or downside]
- [Another risk]

**Cost:** [Money, time, effort, opportunity cost]

### Option B: [Name it]

**What this means:** [One sentence]

**Arguments for:**
- [Reason]
- [Reason]

**Arguments against:**
- [Risk]
- [Risk]

**Cost:** [Money, time, effort, opportunity cost]

### Option C: [Name it — optional]

[Same structure. Only add a third option if it's genuinely distinct, not just a compromise between A and B.]

---

## What I'm leaning toward

[Your gut feeling before analysis. Write this down first — it's useful to compare against your conclusion after thinking it through.]

---

## Analysis

### What would have to be true for each option to be the right call?

- Option A is right if: [condition]
- Option B is right if: [condition]
- Option C is right if: [condition]

### What am I most worried about?

[The risk that keeps you up at night. Name it directly.]

### What information would make this decision easier?

[What don't you know that you wish you did? Is it worth finding out before deciding, or would that just be procrastination?]

## Decision

**Chosen option:** [A / B / C]

**Reasoning:** [2-3 sentences on why. This is for your future self who will read this in 6 months and need to remember the logic.]

**Next steps:**
1. [First action]
2. [Second action]
3. [Third action]

**Review date:** [When will you check if this was the right call? Set a concrete date.]

## How to use this

**Option 1: Fill it in yourself.** Think through the decision on paper, then ask Claude to challenge your reasoning: "Here's my decision document. Play the sceptic — what am I missing?"

**Option 2: Co-create with Claude.** Tell Claude the decision you're facing and ask it to walk you through the template: "I need to decide whether to [X]. Help me think through it." Claude will ask questions and fill in the analysis.

**Option 3: Use thinking moves.** For high-stakes decisions, combine this template with the thinking moves from your Cookbook:
- **Show Your Working** — ask Claude to reason through each option out loud
- **The Sceptic's Chair** — ask Claude to argue against your preferred option
- **Remove the Easy Answer** — take the obvious choice off the table and see what emerges

---

## Notes

- The "what would have to be true" question is the most powerful part. It turns vague preferences into testable conditions.
- Writing down your gut feeling first prevents you from rationalising toward whatever the analysis says. If your conclusion disagrees with your gut, that's worth examining.
- Not every decision needs this template. If you can decide in under 5 minutes, just decide. This is for the ones where the cost of getting it wrong justifies the time to think it through.
