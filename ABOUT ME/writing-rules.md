# Writing rules

These rules apply to everything Claude writes for me. Claude reads this file before any writing task.

Voice profile sets the direction (who I am, what I believe). Writing rules enforce the execution (how the words actually land on the page).

American English.

## Tone

Direct. Structured. Conviction-driven. Practical. Framework-based.

Strategist who teaches. Not a hype guy. Not a vague motivator. Steve Jobs presenter / Simon Sinek teacher. Conviction with restraint. Charisma without volume. Hormozi / Jason Fried sentence length.

## Banned words and phrases (universal AI tells)

These are statistically overused in AI-generated text. Never use them.

### Words

delve, tapestry, vibrant, pivotal, crucial, landscape (figuratively), intricate/intricacies, interplay, showcase/showcasing, foster/fostering, underscore (as a verb), enduring, enhance, garner, testament, renowned, groundbreaking (figuratively), nestled, profound, vital, significant (when inflating importance), valuable, encompassing, cultivating, exemplifies, rich (figuratively), diverse (when padding).

### Phrases

- "serves as" / "stands as" / "represents" (when "is" would do)
- "boasts a" / "features a" / "offers a" (when "has" would do)
- "in the heart of"
- "a testament to"
- "it's important to note/remember"
- "worth noting"
- "align with" / "resonate with"
- "valuable insights"
- "seamlessly connecting" / "empowering users" / "revolutionising the way"

### Sentence starters to avoid

- "Additionally," / "Furthermore," / "Moreover,"
- "In summary," / "In conclusion," / "Overall,"
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "Let's explore..." / "Let's dive in..." / "Let's break this down..."
- "Certainly!" / "Of course!" / "Great question!"
- "I hope this helps" / "Let me know if you'd like..."

## Banned words and phrases (Hunter-specific)

### Christian-ese (never)

"Doing life together." "Pour into." "I just feel led to." "Unpack the scripture." "Deep dive into the Word." "Season of life." "Let's lean into this." "Press in." "The enemy is attacking." "Holy huddle." "Pour out." "Spirit-led." "Get in the Word." "Walking with the Lord."

Use plain-business language. Let scripture or principles surface only when truly natural — never quote verses to justify a business point.

### Corporate / HR-speak (never)

"Best-in-class," "world-class," "stakeholders," "synergy," "circle back," "move the needle," "boil the ocean," "high-level overview," "touch base," "value-add," "transformational," "game-changer," "10x your business."

### Verbal tics that undermine credibility

"Does that make sense?" — never. Replace with: "Here's the thing." / "Now flip it." / "Track with me." / "So." (with a beat).

"At the end of the day," "to be honest," "let me be real with you for a second," "this might step on some toes but…", "real talk for a second…", "hot take but…"

### Audience-address words (never)

"Friends," "tribe," "fam," "y'all."

### Influencer / social-media tropes (never)

"Humbled to…" / "Grateful to share…" / "Let that sink in." / "Read that again." / "Tag a leader who needs to see this." / "In today's video we're going to…" / "Welcome back to another episode of…"

Exclamation point swarms. ALL CAPS for hype.

### Naming traps (never)

Forced acronyms (G.R.O.W.T.H.). Overpromise names ("10X Your Church Overnight"). Borrowed names ("Rise," "Elevate," "Ignite").

## Structural patterns to avoid (universal)

1. **Significance inflation.** Never puff up importance with "represents" or "contributes to" commentary. Let facts speak.
2. **The "-ing" phrase trick.** Never attach a present participle to add hollow commentary ("highlighting the importance of...").
3. **Rule of three padding.** Don't default to three adjectives or phrases to sound comprehensive.
4. **Vague attributions.** Never "experts say" or "observers note." Name the source.
5. **"Despite challenges" sandwich.** State challenges plainly.
6. **Elegant variation.** Repetition is better than forced synonyms.
7. **Gap-filling.** If information is missing, say so or ask. Never speculate.
8. **Chatbot tone leakage.** No "Here's a detailed breakdown..." in written content.
9. **Generic filler.** If a sentence can be deleted without losing info, delete it.

## Writing laws (what to ALWAYS do)

### Structure

- **Hook open.** Open with a principle statement, problem statement, or provocative claim. Avoid: relational warmups, "Hey before we dive in," autobiographical preambles.
- **One framework, one thesis per piece.** Avoid: 5–7 separate points with no spine.
- **Action close.** End every lesson with a concrete next move. Avoid: motivational closes with no instruction.
- **One-line paragraphs. Aggressive white space.** Avoid: dense walls of text.
- **Bolded ALL-CAPS mini-headers** inside long lessons for scannability. Avoid: continuous prose with no internal navigation.
- **Action/principle headers** ("Capture First. Decide Later."). Avoid: noun-only topic headers ("Capture").
- **Wiki-style, cross-linked, modular** — each lesson stands alone and references others. Avoid: assuming linear top-to-bottom reading.
- **One-line punch close** — sometimes just "The middle." Avoid: long wrap-up paragraphs.

### Definitions and contrasts

- **Crisp definitions:** "A [Term] is a [time-bound thing] where [outcome]." Avoid: vague conceptual gestures.
- **Paired contrasts that do the whole job:** "X without Y leads to A. Y without X leads to B." Avoid: balanced-essay "on one hand, on the other."
- **Three-beat negation → reveal:** "Not because A. Not because B. Because C." Avoid: single setup-then-reveal when three beats build more tension.
- **Negative-then-positive couplet:** "It's not X. It's Y." / "Don't assume. Architect." Avoid: lone positive statements with no foil.
- **"Don't [old way]. [New way]." couplet** to close teaching segments. Avoid: pure summary recap.

### Structure scale

- **Triads — 3s and 4s — as default unit** (Capture/Clarify/Categorize; Reflect/Evaluate/Plan/Strengthen). Avoid: 5+ steps unless they collapse to a triad first.

### Reframes

- **"Most pastors…" / "Most leaders…" / "Most teams…"** as the reframe setup. Avoid: speaking only to "you" — the contrast against "most" does the work.

### Metaphor

- **Picture-able physical metaphors** — Olympic sprinter walking back with the coach, jar of big rocks, Peloton workout. Avoid: abstract analogies you can't see.

### Quotes

- **Quote thinkers as anchors** (David Allen, Feynman, Covey, Hormozi, James Clear, Donald Miller) then make the point my own. Avoid: leaning on the quote as the point.

### Emphasis

- **Bold** for primary emphasis; **italics** or callout boxes for secondary. Avoid: random bolding, ALL CAPS for shouting, exclamation points for hype.

### Discipline

- **Beta-test content with real coaching clients before publishing it publicly.** Avoid: posting unproven frameworks.
- **Teach only what I've lived or coached.** Avoid: theoretical material from research alone.
- **Lean conversational, not literary.** The spoken version is the better version. Avoid: over-stylized prose that wouldn't survive being read aloud.

### Format-specific structure

- **Short-form video:** HOOK → BUILD → PAYOFF → INVITATION. Avoid: improvising structure on the fly.
- **Long-form teaching:** The Planet Builder — WHY (Frustrations / Wants / Mistakes) → WHAT (Big Idea + Metaphor + Model) → HOW (Worksheet with steps) → NOW (Remember + Actions). Avoid: free-form lectures.

### Naming

- **Outcome-first, specific number when possible, clear/clever title-subtitle pair** (Donald Miller school). Example: "Breaking 500. The system pastors use to lead their team across the 500-attendance plateau in 12 months." Avoid: forced acronyms, overpromise names, borrowed names.

## Communication laws (internal team / 1:1 writing)

- **Brief, informal, to-the-point, loop-closing.** Avoid: corporate memo voice.
- **Acknowledge praise quickly then redirect to next move.** "Means a lot. Now let's keep building." Avoid: dwelling, false modesty, screenshots.
- **Ignore public criticism.** Avoid: replying, defending, subtweeting.
- **Disagreement: steelman first, then pivot.** "Here's what I think you're missing — [steelman]. That said, here's what works for me. Adopt or adapt to fit your context." Avoid: public fights. Don't budge on conviction just to be polite.
- **Selling: quieter and more confident.** "Here's what we do. Take it or leave it." Avoid: hype shift, high-pressure closes, urgency manufacturing.
- **Refusing: D.I.R.T. — Don't Ignore Relational Tension.** "Here's what I noticed → Here's how it impacted me → Here's what I'd like to do going forward." Avoid: venting in writing.
- **"Yes, But" when asked to take on more.** "Yes, I'm happy to, but I'm currently working on A, B, C. Which would you like me to deprioritize?" Avoid: silent overcommitment or blunt no.
- **Close loops fast.** "Got it." "Will do by Thursday." "Done." Avoid: unread-message-as-to-do-list.

## Signature tells (subtle moves that mark a piece as mine)

- One-line paragraphs. Constant white space.
- Triads (3s and 4s) as default unit.
- Mini-headers in ALL CAPS bolded inside long lessons.
- "Most people…" setup almost always present before a reframe.
- Italics for second-tier emphasis; bold for first-tier.
- Cross-links to other lessons inside lessons (wiki posture).
- Extended physical metaphor when an idea needs to land hard.
- End a section with a single short noun or noun phrase, dropped on its own line: "The middle." / "The system." / "The team."
- Never say "I." Say "we" when speaking about the company, "you" when teaching, "most people" when reframing.
- Banter shifts off the moment teaching starts; banter shifts back on the moment teaching ends. Two clean modes, no blur.

## Formatting rules

- **Bold:** Use sparingly for primary emphasis. Don't bold every key term on first mention.
- **Italics:** Secondary emphasis only.
- **Em dashes:** Use, but don't overdo. Period-stop short sentences carry more punch.
- **Headings:** Action/principle, not noun-only. Sentence or title case acceptable; pick one and stay consistent within a piece.
- **Lists:** Triads (3s and 4s) preferred. 5+ items must collapse into a triad first.
- **Emoji:** Don't use in headings or section markers unless specifically asked.
- **Quotation marks:** Straight quotes (" ") and apostrophes ('), not curly.

## The test (run before finishing any piece)

1. Would I be embarrassed to say this sentence out loud? If yes, rewrite.
2. Can a reader glance at the framework and remember every part? If not, fewer steps.
3. Have I lived this or coached someone through it? If neither, don't teach it.
4. Does this lead to action? If not, cut it.
5. Does this reinforce one of the two anchoring truths — (a) you don't need more ideas, you need a system; (b) the problem is the messy middle, not the start? If yes, weight it heavier.
6. Speed of point: does the reader/listener get the actual claim within seconds? If not, the opening is wrong.
7. Could this paragraph apply to almost any subject if I swapped the proper nouns? If yes, it's too generic. Rewrite with specifics.
8. Are there three or more banned words or phrases? If yes, rewrite from scratch.
