# Competitor site analysis

> This is an example output — a research deliverable Claude produced for the Website Redesign project. Outputs live inside the project folder they belong to, keeping briefs, memory, and deliverables together in one place.

---

## Summary

Reviewed three competitor sites saved in Notion. Key patterns worth borrowing, and mistakes worth avoiding.

---

## Studio A — studioalpha.com

**What works:** Hero section uses one sentence and a full-bleed project image. No navigation clutter. Loads in 1.8 seconds. Portfolio uses a simple grid with hover-to-reveal project names — clean and confident.

**What doesn't:** No clear CTA above the fold. The "Contact" link is buried in the footer. Blog hasn't been updated in 9 months, which signals neglect.

**Steal this:** The single-sentence hero. It forces clarity about what you actually do.

---

## Studio B — bravobranding.co

**What works:** Strong social proof — client logos immediately visible, three short testimonials on the homepage. Pricing is transparent (starter / growth / custom tiers). Contact form is short — just name, email, and "tell us about your project."

**What doesn't:** The site is slow (4.2 seconds on mobile). Too many fonts. The portfolio pages are image-heavy with no context about what the project achieved.

**Steal this:** The short contact form and upfront pricing. Removes friction for people who are comparing agencies.

---

## Studio C — charliecreative.design

**What works:** Mobile experience is excellent — clearly designed mobile-first. Portfolio case studies include the brief, process, and results (including metrics). The "About" page actually has personality.

**What doesn't:** Homepage tries to do too much — services, portfolio, testimonials, blog posts, newsletter signup, and a podcast embed all on one page. It's overwhelming. Also no clear visual hierarchy — everything competes for attention.

**Steal this:** The case study format (brief → process → results with metrics). This is what makes a portfolio persuasive, not just pretty.

---

## Recommendations for our redesign

The strongest sites nail three things: speed, clarity, and social proof. Our redesign should prioritise a fast-loading single-page layout (like Studio A), transparent next steps (like Studio B's short contact form), and case studies that show results, not just visuals (like Studio C). Avoid the common trap of cramming everything onto the homepage.
