# A-Team Workshop — Core Frameworks

*Built using the RSG Framework Architect process. Four frameworks for the workshop's loose sections — Hiring (3) and Bonus #2: Levels.*

---

# FRAMEWORK 1 — HIRE FOR THE FIVE

*The Non-Negotiables That Predict a Healthy Hire*

## THE PROBLEM THIS SOLVES

Most pastors hire on gut, gifting, and availability — then spend the next two years trying to fix a hire they should have caught in week one.

---

## THE FRAMEWORK

**1. Score Competency. Not Potential.**
A 5C interview scores whether someone has *demonstrated* the skill — not whether you *believe* they could.
*Replaces:* hiring on charisma, story, or vibes instead of evidence.

**2. Read Character. Below the Resume.**
Character is the pattern in their private life that nobody is asking about.
*Replaces:* assuming a strong testimony equals strong character.

**3. Test Chemistry. With the Team, Not Just You.**
Chemistry means the existing team gets better when this person walks in the room.
*Replaces:* pastor-only chemistry checks that miss what the staff already sees.

**4. Project Capacity. Forward, Not Backward.**
Capacity is whether they can grow into the next version of this role — not whether they can handle today's version.
*Replaces:* hiring people who'll be maxed out before the church grows.

**5. Check Culture. Before the Contract.**
Culture is whether they already live your values — not whether they can adapt to them.
*Replaces:* hiring "good people" who then need to be rewired.

---

## THE PAIRED CONTRAST

> *"Skill without character builds a ministry that breaks at the worst possible time. Character without skill builds a ministry that breaks slowly — and quietly."*

---

## THE DIAGNOSTIC QUESTION

> *"When you think about your last three hires — which C did you not actually score before you offered them the job?"*

---

## THE PROOF POINT

You know this is working when you can tell within 90 days whether a hire was right — and the reason matches one of the five C's, not a vague feeling.

---

## RSG FIT CHECK

- **Chain link:** *clarity* (on who fits before you hire) and *role clarity* (the Five become the basis for evaluation later).
- **Core truth:** "You don't need more ideas. You need a system that actually gets your team executing." This is the system that protects the team before the team forms.
- **Fits cleanly.**

---

## THE PUNCH CLOSE

> *"Hire the person you already know you have."*

# FRAMEWORK 2 — THE PREFERRED PROFILE

*Define the Hire Before You Interview the Candidates*

## THE PROBLEM THIS SOLVES

Most pastors interview before they've defined the role — so they end up hiring the best candidate in the room instead of the right candidate for the church.

---

## THE FRAMEWORK

**1. Name the Pain First. Then the Role.**
The Preferred Profile starts with one sentence describing what's broken without this person.
*Replaces:* writing job descriptions before diagnosing what the hire actually solves.

**2. Draft the 365 Before You Hire.**
Write the 3 cycle goals, 6 responsibilities, and 5 values *for the role* — before you post or interview.
*Replaces:* hiring someone, then making up their job after they start.

**3. Split the Page. Their Side and Yours.**
One column says who you think you need. The other column gets handed to the candidate so they tell you who they think they are.
*Replaces:* interviews where you do all the assuming and they do all the performing.

**4. Set the Red Flags. In Writing. In Advance.**
Red flags decided emotionally in the moment become hires you regret. Decide them before the first interview.
*Replaces:* emotional rationalization of warning signs you saw on day one.

**5. Connect the Profile to the Matrix.**
The Preferred Profile feeds the scorecard. Every C you'll score later traces back to a line on this page.
*Replaces:* scoring candidates against a standard nobody wrote down.

---

## THE PAIRED CONTRAST

> *"A clear candidate without a clear profile leads to a hire that fits the room but not the role. A clear profile without a clear candidate leads to an empty seat — which is almost always better than the wrong seat filled."*

---

## THE DIAGNOSTIC QUESTION

> *"If I asked you to write the 365 of your next hire — before you've met them — could you do it in under 30 minutes?"*

---

## THE PROOF POINT

You know this is working when the hire's first 90 days match what was written on the Profile — not what was discovered after they started.

---

## RSG FIT CHECK

- **Chain link:** *clarity* — the upstream act of defining what "right" looks like before you hire.
- **Core truth:** "You don't need more ideas. You need a system that actually gets your team executing." The Profile is the system that prevents the wrong hire from breaking the team in the middle.
- **Fits cleanly.**

---

## THE PUNCH CLOSE

> *"Define the seat before you fill it."*

# FRAMEWORK 3 — THE 90+ RULE

*The Hiring Scorecard That Forces Honesty Before You Offer*

## THE PROBLEM THIS SOLVES

Most pastors make their final hiring decision in the parking lot after the interview — based on a feeling — and then spend the next two years scoring the hire they should have scored before the offer.

---

## THE FRAMEWORK

**1. Score the Five C's. Score Five More.**
The Matrix takes the 5 C's and adds five behavioral checks — Calling, Coachability, Conviction, Consistency, Confidence — for ten total. Each scored 1–10.
*Replaces:* hiring on five filters when you actually have ten signals.

**2. Set the Floor. Below a 7 Is a No.**
One score below 7 in any category = automatic no, even if the total clears 90. This is the discipline most leaders skip.
*Replaces:* high overall scores hiding a non-negotiable red flag.

**3. Weight Character and Culture Twice.**
These two are non-negotiables. Score them double. A 60 on character means it doesn't matter how high the rest scores.
*Replaces:* treating every category as equal when two of them aren't.

**4. Hit 90. Or Hold the Seat.**
Below 90, you don't hire. You wait. The cost of the wrong hire is always higher than the cost of an empty seat.
*Replaces:* settling for "good enough" because the seat has been open too long.

**5. Score in Writing. Sign in Pen.**
The matrix lives on paper. Everyone who interviews signs their own. No verbal scoring. No memory-based decisions.
*Replaces:* collective amnesia and revisionist scoring after the fact.

---

## THE PAIRED CONTRAST

> *"A high score without a clear filter leads to confident hires you'll regret. A clear filter without a real score leads to slow hires you'll second-guess. You need both — the standard and the discipline to apply it."*

---

## THE DIAGNOSTIC QUESTION

> *"If I handed you a scorecard for your last hire — could you fill it out honestly right now? And would they hit 90?"*

---

## THE PROOF POINT

You know this is working when "I just have a feeling about them" stops being a reason — and the number on the page becomes the conversation.

---

## RSG FIT CHECK

- **Chain link:** *clarity* (decision clarity before the offer) and *focused priorities* (refusing to fill a seat that won't move the cycle forward).
- **Core truth:** "You don't need more ideas. You need a system that actually gets your team executing." The Matrix is the system that protects every hire from emotional drift.
- **Fits cleanly.**

---

## THE PUNCH CLOSE

> *"Score it. Or you'll suffer it."*

# FRAMEWORK 4 — THE 4 LEVELS

*Where Every Staff Member Is Actually Operating Right Now*

## THE PROBLEM THIS SOLVES

Most pastors evaluate their staff by output — but the real ceiling on the team isn't what people produce, it's the leadership level they're operating from.

---

## THE FRAMEWORK

**1. Diagnose Level 1: They Don't Feel the Weight.**
Level 1 leaders have ideas, no experience, and no carrying. They critique freely because they don't carry anything.
*Replaces:* confusing energy and opinion for leadership.

**2. Diagnose Level 2: They Feel the Weight.**
Level 2 leaders carry real responsibility — and feel the anxiety of it. This is where most of your staff lives, and where most of them quietly burn out.
*Replaces:* assuming someone who is stressed is failing. They're usually just at Level 2.

**3. Diagnose Level 3: They Take the Weight to God.**
Level 3 leaders carry just as much as Level 2 — but they've learned to hand it off daily. The output looks calmer because the dependency is deeper.
*Replaces:* misreading peace as low performance. Calm Level 3 leaders are often more productive than frantic Level 2 leaders.

**4. Diagnose Level 4: They Carry the Weight So Others Can Become.**
Level 4 leaders aren't measured by what they carry. They're measured by who they make able to carry.
*Replaces:* keeping your most senior leaders in Level 2 work because nobody trained them up.

**5. Coach to the Next Level. Not the Next Task.**
Every 1:1 should answer one question: what does this person need to grow into the next level — not just finish the next assignment?
*Replaces:* developing skill while neglecting leadership maturity.

---

## THE PAIRED CONTRAST

> *"Level without weight produces leaders who critique what they've never carried. Weight without level produces leaders who carry what they were never designed to carry alone."*

---

## THE DIAGNOSTIC QUESTION

> *"Pick three names on your staff. What level is each one operating from this cycle — and what's one specific thing you've done in the last 30 days to move them up?"*

---

## THE PROOF POINT

You know this is working when staff conversations stop sounding like *"are they doing their job?"* and start sounding like *"what level are they on, and what's the next move?"*

---

## RSG FIT CHECK

- **Chain link:** *role clarity* (the level shapes what success looks like) and *clarity* (you stop misdiagnosing the same staff problem cycle after cycle).
- **Core truth:** "The problem isn't starting strong. It's not falling apart in the middle." Most teams fall apart because Level 2 leaders never get coached to Level 3 — and burn out in the middle.
- **Fits cleanly.**

---

## THE PUNCH CLOSE

> *"Level beats effort. Every time."*
